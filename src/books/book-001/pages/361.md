A multi-element album page combining wedding photos, a newspaper portrait, and three envelopes from family correspondents.

**Upper left — newspaper clipping (wedding portrait):**

A large half-length clipping of a young bride in a long lace mantilla veil, posed in profile against a dark backdrop. The caption beneath, partly obscured by mounting, reads:

> With an antique lace [veil], Miss Marie Walton Beckett, of [Edisto Island], wore the wedding gown of her [grandmother], Marie [Walton], of [Edisto Island], when she became the bride of [Mr.] Donald McKay Allston, third, son of Mr. and Mrs. Donald McKay Allston, junior, of [Bonneau]. She was given in marriage by her father, Mr. James Augustine Beckett, junior. *[Caption partly trimmed at mount edge.]*

**Upper center — two black-and-white snapshots:**

Snapshot at the upper edge: a bride and groom photographed outdoors, the groom in dark suit, the bride in a knee-length veil holding flowers. Pencil caption:

> Vera & Mother

Snapshot below it: a young couple smiling at the camera. Pencil caption:

> Donald & [Vera]

**Lower left — newspaper clipping:**

A clipping from *The News and Courier*, Sunday, Feb. 18, 1962, page 7-A, headed:

> 4 Men Promoted At C. & S. Bank

Beside a head-and-shoulders portrait of a young man in a dark suit, captioned **H. M. Allston Jr., Home Sales Officer**. The body of the article reports the promotion of four men at the Charleston office of the Citizens & Southern National Bank of S.C. from several departments. *[Body text largely legible but small; names include H. M. Allston Jr. as Home Sales Officer.]*

**Right side — three overlapping envelopes:**

*Top envelope (upper right):* a long white business envelope, postmarked Chicago, addressed in typescript:

> From: Donald Allston 4053
> 19 B Bartow-Jackson
> U.S. Naval Training School
> University of Chicago
> Chicago 37, Illinois
>
> Mrs. J. P. Walker
> Mrs. Donald Allston
> Bonneau Island
> South Carolina

*Middle envelope:* a folded sheet showing a small printed return-address block at the upper left:

> Horace W. Phillips
> Hardeeville, S.C.

Addressed in blue ink:

> Mrs. J. P. Walker
> 3648 Hedrick St.
> Jacksonville
> Florida

A pencil note in the right margin reads:

> Letter from Mrs. Horace Phillips — March 14, 1959 — Telling this is the old surname Allston by H. old surname [illegible], [Charlotte] Allston.

*Lower envelope:* a torn long envelope, addressed in blue fountain pen:

> Mrs. J. P. Walker
> 3648 Hedrick St.
> Jacksonville,
> Florida

Bearing a purple 3-cent Statue of Liberty stamp and a circular postmark.
