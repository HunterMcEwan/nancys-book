She concludes praising the book as 'an incontrovertible testimony to the much misunderstood & often maligned character of our people & civilization,' and expresses pleasure that the Charleston Library Society, the South Carolina Historical Society and the College of Charleston are associated with a work of this magnitude. She wishes that 'Nathaniel Wright Stephenson could have lived to rejoice in it'; Stephenson (1867–1935) was a Charleston-connected historian and professor at the College of Charleston, author of works on Abraham Lincoln and the Old South.
