> Hendersonville N.C.
> Wednesday April 1, 1925
>
> Dear Sister,
>
> I hate to get [in advance] of [Brenden] [Walker?] the May 7th congratulations for the well deserved tribute of the L.L.D. degree, for such an able devotion to the Charleston Library — and your loyal interest in the [College] and helpfulness to the students. — Doctor does not let me forget it makes me realize what a slim margin there is between us.
>
> You are very fortunate in not having an "up-to-date" photograph of yourself for the New York Camera Club. Theodore Jervis [leaves] him to be "out of date."
>
> It is wonderful the progress you have made for only two years ago you were taken for a mendicant! Lily and I are to walk with dignity and pride and stand trotting on the streets, specially to and from the Library. — You [never] knew Pride could push before a [fall]. — [Robison] Reg, Frank and his family, and I are no end, [Theodore] and Cleo as visit. I was at Hugh [Barnwell's] when they arrived and [enjoyed] them with [them] for the day. We also had Dada for the week-end and it was a great pleasure to see us all join. Theodore tell me that he sees nearly all their children and to go to-[day] with them [Dunbar] and Margie [Trumbull]
