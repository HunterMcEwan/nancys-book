A full newspaper page from **The News and Courier, Charleston, S.C., Sunday Morning, March 26, 1961**, page **5-C** (continued on **6-C**).

## Top headline (across page)

> **Water Key To Progress In South, Group Is Told**
>
> COLUMBIA (Special) — "Water is the key to progress in South Carolina — whether it is industrial or agricultural," the South Carolina Agricultural Public Relations Council was told at its recent meeting in Columbia.
>
> This was brought out in a tour of inspection of the work of the Department of Water in South Carolina…

[Article continues across two columns describing a tour for the Council on water resources.]

## Photograph caption (centre-left)

A black-and-white photograph of four young men kneeling in a field, one demonstrating a soil-sampling tool. Caption beneath:

> 4-H CLUB MEMBERS JIMMY MULLINAX AND WADE KING TAKE SAMPLES
> County Agent H. D. Harrell, Cattleman J. R. Wingo, Teacher Fred Morgan Look On

## Lower-left headline

> **In Anderson County Area**
>
> **Soil Testing Program Is Paying Off**
>
> CLEMSON (Special) — "Soil testing has been following a regular pattern of accuracy and reliability," said Mr. Wingo. "Farmers have not had bad fertilizer years lately but most have changed to using a more efficient fertilizer recommended by the program…"

[Article continues describing soil-testing results in Anderson County.]
