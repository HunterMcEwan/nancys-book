> **The Times-News, Hendersonville, N.C., Sat., December 8, 1979**
>
> # Mr. Fitz — Henderson County's Master Storyteller
>
> **By BOB WHITMIRE**
>
> Frank L. FitzSimons Sr. is more than the author of three volumes of local lore and tall tales. He is also a schoolteacher, soldier, banker, football coach, broadcast journalist and winner of the Navy Cross during World War I.
>
> But more than these things, Frank FitzSimons is a storyteller — one in the tradition of storytellers who have escaped a passing of centuries around the supper fires of countless generations.
>
> "As a child I spent my summers walking these mountains, talking to people and gathering folk stories and folk lore," FitzSimons said during a recent interview.
>
> He moved to Hendersonville permanently in 1921, and as a school teacher in the venerable High Shoals FitzSimons continued to walk the ridges and coves, picking up bits and pieces of local lore.
>
> Then in the 1930s, while he was an executive with the old Ham Trust Co. (now Northwestern Bank), he was talked into going on WHKP Radio to replace a regular program that the bank had been sponsoring. "They told me I would do this for a year, more or less. I think it is now thirty-eight years!" FitzSimons said with a smile.
>
