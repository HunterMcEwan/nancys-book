A composite page of portraits and a hand-drawn family tree, with a newspaper clipping pasted at the upper right.

**Upper left — a large oval albumen portrait of a clean-shaven young man in dark suit, hair parted on the right, mounted in an embossed cardboard surround.**

*[Uncaptioned. Apparently a member of the Coffin or Perry families named in the tree below.]*

**Upper right — newspaper clipping. Folded headline strip across the top reads:**

> MARCH 28, 1954 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PAGE 47

Headline:

> **Distinctive Churches of Florida**

Beneath the headline a small article header:

> **Mr. D. P. Coffin**

The body of the article (partly visible — continued onto page 621) reads:

> An esteemed, loyal and useful citizen of Wakulla \[? — Wakulla County], Mr. Dunkin Perry Coffin, 71, passed away in the United States Hospital of \[?] Florida afternoon after a few days of acute illness.
>
> Born at Orangeburg county, S. C., on April 19, 1857, Mr. Coffin was a son of the late Mr. William Bee Coffin and his wife, Elizabeth Howell, of Charleston, S. C. Born in Charleston in 1881 he married Elizabeth Perry. After ten years spent with that firm, he became a [retail] hardware merchant, having been connected with various firms in his line of business: the Hardware Co. Reynolds Co. He was a long time member of the Knights of Pythias, having a lifelong connection of A. F. & A. M. Episcopal Church.

*[The clipping continues onto page 621.]*
