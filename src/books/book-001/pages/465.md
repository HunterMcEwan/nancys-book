  Fifth sheet of the SGS genealogy packet — a hand-drawn pedigree chart in blue ink with pencil annotations, numbered '5' (encircled) at upper right. Shows the descent of the Gaillard family from Jean Gaillard and Anne Gervaise down through several generations, splitting into a left branch (the Gourdin / Haydon Hill / Stoney line that leads to SGS himself) and a right branch (the Barker / FitzSimons line that leads to the compiler Amy FitzSimons Walker). Marriages are shown with horizontal pairings; descents drop in freehand vertical lines and brackets. A dark blot in the right column partially obscures the line 'Samuel Gaillard Barker / m. [Ellen Milliken]'. A small marginal pencil annotation between the columns reads 'Henrietta Catherine Barker / m. 2nd Stathie Lee' (cursive uncertain — possibly 'Stathe' or 'Statee').

  Editorial note: this is the working pedigree underlying the numbered outline on pages 463–464. Key proper names: **Anne Gervaise**; **Ellinor** Cordes; **Theodore Gourdin** (Charleston Huguenot merchant, 1764–1826, m. Elizabeth Gaillard 1785); **Peter Gaillard of Haydon Hill** (Haydon Hill Plantation); **Joseph Sanford Barker** (Henrietta Catherine Gaillard's husband, sometimes recorded as "Joseph Sanford Barlow / Barker"); **Susan Barker**. The chart visually unifies two descents that converge in the compiler's grandfather Dr. Christopher FitzSimons (3rd): one through his wife Susan Milliken Barker (right column), and Susan's mother Henrietta Catherine Gaillard (Joseph Sanford Barker's wife). The left column traces the SGS compiler's own line down through Harriet Porcher Gaillard m. Samuel David Stoney. The marginal "m. 2nd Stathie Lee" annotation marks a second marriage for the daughter Henrietta Catherine Barker — surname Lee, given name uncertain; the cursive "Stathie" is the closest plain reading but could be Statee, Stathe, Stithe, or a similar variant.
