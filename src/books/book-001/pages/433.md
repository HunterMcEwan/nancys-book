A clipped newspaper article mounted on the album page. No dateline or masthead is visible.

**Upper photograph caption (bold):**

> TEACHER RICHARD WRIGHT AND CHRIS McEWAN
>
> *…with coral snake 38 inches long*

**Kicker:**

> Shakes Delayed

**Headline:**

> **Carpenter Kills Huge Coral Snake**

**Article body:**

> Frank Keim had a delayed case of the shakes today and [just] because he killed a 38-inch coral snake yesterday.
>
> "I don't think I would have been around here very long if I had known it was a coral snake. I hate all snakes," he claimed.
>
> Keim, 29 E. Winter Park Ave., a carpenter working at the home of O. B. McEwan, 1129 S. Osceola St., said he noticed the reptile in the McEwan driveway but didn't pay much attention to it.
>
> He later told Mrs. McEwan, and she asked him to kill it. He took his hatchet, chased the snake out of some bushes, then killed it.
>
> The McEwan family had an argument about what kind of snake it was because McEwan didn't believe a coral snake grew 38 inches long.
>
> Chris McEwan, 13-year-old eighth-grader, took the reptile to Cherokee Junior High School and science teacher Richard Wright now has it on display in his classroom.
>
> Dr. Paul Vestal, professor of biology at Rollins College, said he has heard of a 40-inch coral snake but "38 inches is about as big as they ever get."
>
> Coral snakes normally reach a 30-36 inch length at maturity. The record for Florida is 49 inches.

**Lower inset photograph:** A small head-shot of the carpenter, captioned **Keim**.
