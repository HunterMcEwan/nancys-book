The closing sheet of the cursive birthday letter. The handwriting is consistent with pages 426 and 428.

> a few new potatoes I had to keep him a part of the way. He loves to come over to see me, always wants flowers, and I generally give him some that are not very fine, and let him take a pretty bunch to his mother. He always suggests that Mommie has been in grandaddie's drawer, but gets me to look in the drawer — just as he does — not wanting to leave any sudden encounter with the snake and crow. Anyway, what is now — it will [be], some one has gone and made it pretty clear that the way is safe. They are all in Savannah for the week, and I am missing them a great deal.
>
> Uncle Seaman came in unexpectedly on Friday for a week's stay, and we are all enjoying having him. He is in the finest feeling just now. Give my love to all the dear ones.
>
> Sincerely,
> *[signature — illegible flourish]*
