A long sheet of lined paper closely written in cursive in brown ink, with verse centered between wide margins.

> In Memoriam:
>
> Behind this Stone is laid,
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For a Season,
>
> Albert Sidney Johnstone,
>
> A General in the Army of the Confederate States,
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Who fell at Shiloh Tennessee,
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the [6]th day of April,
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A. D. 1862;
> A Man tried in many high offices
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And critical enterprises,
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And found faithful in all.
>
> His life was one long sacrifice of interest to conscience;
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And even that life, on a woful Sabbath,
> Did he yield as a holocaust to his country's need.
> Not wholly understood was he while he lived;
> But in his death, his greatness stands confessed
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In a people's tears.
>
> Resolute, Moderate, clear of envy,
> Yet not wanting in that finer ambition which
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Makes men great and pure.
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his honor — impregnable;
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In his Simplicity — Sublime;
> No country e'er had a truer Son — No cause a
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nobler Champion;
> No people a Bolder defender — No principle a
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Purer Victim,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Than
