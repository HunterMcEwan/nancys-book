notes: "Final leaf of the multi-page handwritten letter from Susan M. FitzSimons to Mr. Osborne, numbered '4' at the top. Continues from pages 283–285. She closes with anxieties about heavy travel and board expenses, hopes for a good harvest, sends remembrances to his brother and wife and to 'Mrs Osborne,' notes how strange it seems not to be at Mills River at all this summer, and adds that her daughters Ellen and Gaillie send their remembrances. Signed 'Susan M. FitzSimons.' Several words at line ends remain uncertain."

> 4
>
> to pay and our expenses
> have been so heavy with
> travelling & board that
> I am anxious to
> know what I have
> left. I hope your
> crop is good and
> that you have had
> a good harvest time.
> Remember me to
> your brother & wife
> and to Mrs Osborne.
> It seems very strange
> not to be at Mills River
> at all this summer.
> Ellen & Gaillie send
> their remembrances.
>
> Yours sincerely
>
> Susan M. FitzSimons
