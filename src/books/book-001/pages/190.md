An open-book scan showing two facing leaves of a handwritten letter in brown ink on lined paper. The right leaf is paginated **10** at the top centre. A small pencilled annotation above the first line of the left leaf, in a different hand, reads: *Mrs Huger & Margaret* (apparently identifying "Mary & Hennie" of the next line as Mrs Huger and her companion Margaret — though see also page 191L, where the same names appear separately, so the annotation may simply mark this pair of guests).

## Left column (likely letter page 9)

> Mary & Hennie *[Mrs Huger & Margaret]* / helped to arrange / them. We dined, & Mr Williman / fixed the Supper table, of / course Nell had every / thing *^ready!* put on the Side / table, So she only had the / pleasure of fixing things / on as they liked. — The / flowers were lovely & Such quantities / they were So Many & So / beautifully arranged the / house looked lovely / with lights & all / Grandfathers branch & Silver / Candlesticks were lit / for the occasion & Some / tiny candle Sticks helped / to Soften the gas light — / We got Delphine to come / & take Charge of the / Spare room — where the / ladies could go & leave / their wraps — Some came / in full dress others in walking

## Right column (letter page 10)

> dress, bonnets and no bonnets / as they pleased — It was a / very Successful affair / people Stood & Stood / & talked & Enjoyed it — / Some went down Stairs / & got Refreshment of Cake / wine & ice cream & then / returned to the upper / rooms for another / talk — It is said to be / as Entirely different from / the regular Stiff Receptions / as possible — People of / all Ages were here & / those who have received / cards at a distance have / Some of them written / Notes acknowleding them / & regrets at not being / able to attend — Nell / Says she never heard of / Such politeness as they / are receiving on all Sides
