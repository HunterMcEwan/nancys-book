An open-book scan showing two facing leaves of a handwritten letter in brown ink on lined paper. The right leaf is paginated **10** at the top centre. A small pencilled annotation in another hand sits at the top centre of the left leaf, partly illegible: *Mr [illegible] [illegible]grant*.

## Left column

> Mary & Hennie helped to arrange them. We dined, & Mr. Williman fixed the Supper table, of course Nell had every thing Nancy put on the Side table, So she only had the pleasure of fixing things on as they liked. The flowers were lovely & Such quantities they were So Many & So beautifully arranged the house looked lovely with the lights & all Grandfathers branch & [tile?] Candlesticks were lit for the occasion & Some [tiny] candle Sticks helped to Soften the gas light. We got Delphine to come & take Charge of the Spare room where the ladies could go & leave their wraps — Some came in full dress others in walking

## Right column (page 10)

> dress. bonnets and no bonnets as they pleased. It was a very Successful affair people Stood & Stood & talked & Enjoyed it. Some went down [I room?] Stairs & got refreshment of Cake wine & ice cream & then returned to the upper rooms for another talk — It is said to be as Entirely different from the regular Stiff Receptions as possible — People of all Ages were here & those who have received cards at a distance have Some of them written Notes acknowledging them & regrets at not being able to attend — Nell Says she never heard of Such politeness as they are receiving on all sides
