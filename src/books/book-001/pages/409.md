**Center — engagement notice (clipped column with photo):**

A small bridal head-shot at the top, beside the column heading "**McEwan-Green**":

> ORLANDO — Mr. and M[rs.] Oswald Beverly McEwan announce the engagement of their daughter, Nancy Fletcher, to Theodore Robert Green, Orlando, son of Mr. and Mrs. John Theodore Green, Deerfield Beach.
>
> The wedding will be at noon Feb. 3, in the Cathedral of St. Luke Episcopal Church.
>
> The bride-elect is the granddaughter of Mrs. James Pickens Walker, Jacksonville, and the late Mr. Walker. She attended Ashley Hall, Charleston, S.C., and was graduated from Boone High School and attended the University of Georgia and the Villa Mercede, Florence, Italy. She was presented to society at the Rosalind Club and at the St. Cecelia Ball in Charleston. She is also a member of the Junior League of Orlando.
>
> Her fiance was graduated from East Aurora High in New York, attended Cornell University and received his MBA degree from the University of Miami where he was a member of Sigma Phi Epsilon social fraternity.

**Upper right — Corner Cupboard News feature, February 8, 1968:**

Headed in red banner: **Corner Cupboard NEWS**, February 8, 1968. Below, a half-column bridal portrait of the bride in white veil, fur stole, and chapel-length gown.

> **NANCY McEWAN BECOMES MRS. THEODORE GREEN**
>
> One of the season's most beautiful weddings, both impressive and lovely. The vows said before Father Frank [Casey] Grey were spoken in the Cathedral Episcopal Church of St. Luke. Father Grey, Dean of the Cathedral, officiated.
>
> Nancy's stunning off-white wool ensemble with white mink trim was lovely and so well chosen as a white mink pillbox completed the becoming costume. She held her lovely bouquet of stephanotis blossoms, lilies of the valley, ivy and sweetheart roses. Her two attendants, Amy McEwan, the bride's cousin, and Anne Landon, [illegible] were lovely in pink street-length dresses.
>
> The reception was held in the dining room of the Country Club. A wedding cake of three tiers stood in the center of the buffet, and the bride and groom cut the first slice while photographers and movie men attended. John Theodore Green and Nancy were greeted by the gathered guests.
>
> Following the reception, the bride and groom flew to South America for a honeymoon trip. Upon their return they will make their home in Orlando.
>
> Nancy is the daughter of Mr. and Mrs. O. B. McEwan and a granddaughter of the late Mr. and Mrs. James Pickens Walker, Sr.
>
> They will be at home in 1008 Strathmore, Orlando, after the wedding trip.
>
> Mrs. Theodor G. Green
