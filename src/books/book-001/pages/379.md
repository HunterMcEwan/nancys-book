  An official Certificate of Death issued by the Department of Health, Bureau of Vital Statistics, City of Jacksonville, Florida, for James Pickens Walker (Sr.). Death occurred at the family residence (3698 Hedrick Street, Jacksonville) on the evening of November 19, 1960. Filed November 22, 1960; certified true-and-correct copy bears the seal of the City Health Department and signatures of the City Health Officer and the Director, Division of Vital Statistics (Kathleen Z. Barker). The cause of death is given as acute coronary, with interval between onset and death entered as 'Sudden.' This is the death certificate enumerated on the envelope mounted at page 378. The photostat is dim: key fields read as date of death Nov 19, date of birth Apr 27 1883, age 77, cause of death acute coronary, time of death 9:55 PM, birthplace 'Overfield Com., W. Va.' (i.e., Overfield Community), and mother's maiden name Emma Dee Pickens. The attending physician's signature is rapid and only partially legible (best reading 'MacKenzie Mason' or similar).

  The decedent — Amy FitzSimons's husband — was buried at Philippi, West Virginia, the West Virginia town near his birthplace (Overfield, in present-day Barbour or Greenbrier County); the body was shipped from Jacksonville two days after death. The informant's signature, "A. Walker Jr.", is almost certainly that of his son James Pickens Walker Jr., who lived at the same Jacksonville address.
