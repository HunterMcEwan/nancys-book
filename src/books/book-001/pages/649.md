notes: |
  A typewritten single-sheet affidavit on plain paper, dated
  **28 September 1953, Philippi, W. Va.**, headed "To Whom It May
  Concern". It certifies that the births and deaths listed are copied
  from the family Bible of Mr. and Mrs. James Pickens, Philippi, W. Va.
  The "Relationship of each to" column on the typed sheet originally
  read only "**Mrs. Anna Walker Modisette**", but a handwritten ink
  annotation has been added: **"AND JAMES PICKENS WALKER"** —
  extending the certificate to recognise the compiler's husband
  James Pickens Walker Sr. (1883–1960, the compiler Amy's husband) as
  a relative of the same Pickens line. Anna Walker Modisette has
  signed in pen at the foot of the page; the notarial certificate is
  signed and stamped by **W. D. Corder, Notary Public** (commission
  expiring 15 January 1960).

  Notable details on the affidavit:
  (1) the relationship column is jointly to "Mrs. Anna Walker
  Modisette AND JAMES PICKENS WALKER" (handwritten ink addition over the
  typed line) — the certificate was supplied to BOTH Modisette and to
  Hunter McEwan's grandfather James Pickens Walker Sr., establishing
  the genealogical connection from his side too;
  (2) Fanny Virginia Pickens **d. Feb. 7, 1940**;
  (3) Mollie Pickens **d. Oct. 1, 1949**;
  (4) Alexander Pickens (great-grandfather) — **Apr. 2, 1847** is the
  typed reading on this affidavit, though page 646 gives 1797 (the
  patriarch); the 1847 reading on this typescript appears to be a
  typographical error for 1797 (per p646 the deceased was born long
  before 1747 and died well before 1847);
  (5) the surname **GRITCHFIELD** is handwritten in pencil under
  "James Dever 1788" — Critchfield, the maiden name of Rachel Dever
  (matches p646);
  (6) interlined corrections also give James Dever as "b. 1786,
  78 Yrs. 4 Mo. 28 days" and Rachel Dever (his wife) born **1777**,
  age 57 yrs old at death;
  (7) "Chairty" preserved as misspelled in original (the typist's
  error for "Charity").

A typed single-sheet affidavit on plain paper, with handwritten ink
additions and a notarial stamp at the foot.

*[Typed upper right:]*

> 28 September 1953
> Philippi, W. Va.

*[Typed body:]*

> To Whom It May Concern:
>
> The following record of births and deaths, etc., is copied from the family Bible belonging to Mr. and Mrs. James Pickens, Philippi, W. Va., (James Pickens was the son of Alexander Pickens):

*[Tabular section. Column headings — the rightmost column was originally
typed "Relationship of each to / Mrs. Anna Walker Modisette" but has
been extended by handwritten ink to read "AND JAMES PICKENS WALKER":]*

> | --- | --- | --- | --- |

> | John D. Pickens | Jan. 1, 1845 — Dec. — 1916 | | Uncle |

> Alexander Pickens &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; — Apr. 2, 1847 — Great-Grandfather
>

*[The "Apr. 2, 1847" date on this typescript appears to be a transposition
error for **1797** — page 646 of this same album records Alexander Pickens
as "D. April 2nd 1797", which is genealogically consistent with the
patriarch (great-grandfather) being a generation older than 1817.]*

> W. D. Corder

> My Commission Expires January 15, 1960

*[The handwritten ink addition **"AND JAMES PICKENS WALKER"** to the
relationship-column heading is the key annotation on this document — the
affidavit was prepared for Anna Walker Modisette but the album-compiler
(or whoever obtained the copy on James Pickens Walker Sr.'s behalf) has
annotated it to claim the same kin relationships for him. James Pickens
Walker Sr. (1883–1960) was the compiler Amy FitzSimons Walker's husband
and Hunter McEwan's great-grandfather; his mother Emma Dee Pickens
(1856–1933) — listed in the typed table as "Mother" of Anna Walker
Modisette — was in fact his mother too, making him Modisette's sibling.
The GRITCHFIELD/Critchfield pencil note links to page 646's Dever
genealogy.]*
