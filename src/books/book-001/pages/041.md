A handwritten letter in brown ink on a single page, in flowing cursive.

> Gippy April 21st
>
> My dear Susan
>
> I received your letter yesterday. We are quite disappointed you cannot come up. I have been so little of you that I looked forward with much pleasure to having you here, but your reasons are very good. When I return to C[harleston] Rose to have you a good deal with me. I assure you Susan, I have felt most miserably at times, my dearly loved Jim is seldom out of my thoughts. The last time I saw him was at Monks Corner. My dear Chilen was so affectionate & when taking leave of me was much moved & said so to Mother & if you are in the least [doubtful] send for me & nothing shall keep me away. I feel my [sorrow] too sacred to speak [of] [&] only to those who can feel with me can I ever allude to it — it weighs heavily on my Heart & it requires much effort to struggle against giving vent to my feelings, my earnest prayer is for [submission] to God's will. — No tongue can tell how it distresses me that I can do nothing for you & your dear little ones. Oh how freely how gladly would I assist you if I have it, but I must
