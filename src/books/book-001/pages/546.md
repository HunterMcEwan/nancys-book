notes: |
  Third sheet (numbered "3" at top centre) of the multi-page letter from
  **Gaillard Stoney FitzSimons** ("G.S.F.", Amy's father) to **Amy
  FitzSimons (Mrs. James Pickens Walker)**. Page 2 is p545; page 4 (the
  closing) is p547. Here the writer apologises that his own memory of
  the family history "is faulty and poor" and tells Amy he is sending
  her a **"memorandum of the FitzSimons family"** made by her **"Uncle
  Huger"** — almost certainly the family-history document that is the
  cornerstone of this album. He asks her to return it because he is
  keeping it for his grandchildren, and defers to **"Aunt Ellen"** (his
  sister) as the better keeper of family memory, hoping to get her in
  the mood to write down what she knows about their ancestors.

*[Continuation from page 545 ("2"); continues on page 547 ("4"). "Uncle Huger" — previously read as "Hugen" — is the writer's brother-in-law or uncle whose family memorandum G.S.F. is forwarding; the Hugers were a prominent Charleston family connected to the FitzSimonses by marriage. "Aunt Ellen" is **Ellen FitzSimons**, G.S.F.'s sister.]*
