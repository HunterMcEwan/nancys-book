notes: "First page of a handwritten letter from Wade Hampton at Millwood, dated May 8, 1896, to Major T. G. Barker. Marked 'Personal' in the upper left corner. Hampton writes that the veterans at Houston have put him in command of this department of 'Gordon's Army' and that he must travel to Richmond for the laying of the cornerstone of Mr. Davis' Monument. He wants his old staff with him — Barker as Chief of Staff — and notes that Dr. Taylor and Lewenden will also go. He jokes about Barker practicing horseback riding before directing military movements again. He explains he has been laid up by an inflammation in his leg and is on crutches, but hopes to be on his feet soon when he will go to Washington. The letter continues on the next scan."

> My Dear Adjutant —

> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As you & the other
> veterans at Houston put me in command
> of this Dept. of "Gordon's Army", I must go
> to Richmond when the corner-stone of Mr.
> Davis' Monument will be laid. I want all
> my old staff to be with me, & you as the
> Chief of them. Both Dr. & Tom Taylor will go
> & so will Lewenden, so "if you want to have
> a good time, join the Cavalry". But you
> had better practice riding on horseback
> before you venture again to direct military
> movements! Don't disappoint me, for I wish
> to have not only my old staff, but as
> many of the "Old Brigade" as possible.
