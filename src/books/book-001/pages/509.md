Ellie's hand shows several interlineated revisions inserted small above the line: 'large' inserted before 'mass' (a *large* mass of important materials); 'documents' added above 'materials'; 'indexing' inserted above 'arrangement'; and a struck word ('as' or 'that') before 'the result'. > Dear Mr. Easterby:
>
> I have wanted ever since I received your book to write and thank you for it & for y[ou]r so kind expression of the help that I gave you which was so little compared to what I wished to do, feeling it a great privilege, for I realised what a great opportunity it was to make an outstanding & most important contribution to our history.
>
> You surely have availed yourself of this opportunity bringing all y[ou]r ability, the long stored up knowledge of our history, the tireless energy & [time?] required to sift & render the essentials of a *[large]* mass of important *[documents]* materials enterprising them by careful arrangement, *[indexing]* & most significant notes, so that ~~as that~~ the result is one of the most important contributions
