The writer thanks Easterby for sending his book and acknowledges what a great opportunity it was to make an outstanding contribution to South Carolina history. Ellie inserts interlineated corrections — 'scholar' above 'ability,' 'documents' above 'materials,' and 'indexing' above 'arrangement'. The recipient is almost certainly J. Harold Easterby (1898–1960), Charleston historian and director of the South Carolina Archives, author of A History of the College of Charleston (1935) and editor of The Journal of the Commons House of Assembly. > Dear Mr. Easterby:
>
> I have wanted ever since I received your book to write and thank you for it & for so kind expression of the help that I gave you which was so little compared to what I wished to do, feeling it a great privilege, for I realised what a great opportunity it was to make an outstanding & most important contribution to our history.
>
> You surely have availed yourself of this opportunity bringing all your *[scholar]* ability, the long stored up knowledge of our history, the tireless energy required to sift & render the essentials of a mass of important *[documents]* materials enterprise them by careful arrangement, *[indexing]* & most significant notes; so that ~~as~~ the result is one of the most important contributions
