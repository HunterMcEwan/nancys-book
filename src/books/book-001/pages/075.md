The concluding page of a handwritten letter, in brown ink on lined paper. A struck-through word sits at the top left.

> ~~tho[ugh]t~~ \[?] of my trunks had been Saved & that Peter had them when she came again & they were enquired after. She said she beleived they was \[D]istributed by the Yankees & the next devel-opment is Peter in His Shirts I never let him know that the dishonesty had been detect-ed because he professed faithfulness &c we were making use of him which we would lose & gain nothing by telling him we knew it. Meantime it was very agravating to have to buy cloth & make other shirts & His go ragged for a while — But these are small troubles even to very poor people now Clem is awake again so goodbye
