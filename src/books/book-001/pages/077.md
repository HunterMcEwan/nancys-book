Continuation of the handwritten extract on a torn sheet, the same hand and ink as page 076. Some text at the top and bottom edges is lost.

> [illegible] [illegible] to see you in a very ab\[le] Writer. We acknowledge your influence — we read your Books — we accept your ideal character into our acquaintance and quite take their breaches as we put the Speakers of our friends. We have the highest ad-miration of your genius, your power and your accomplishments, but we do not acknowledge you as an individual. And if you are men you will never build your substance up — Men upon your claims as a Writer. It is a bitter and sad blunder in the experience of many a shipwrecked life. Not even one of us is Man enough in his own home and circle. Let us be sufficiently penetrated to receive that our Work is no [danger] but the work of all others most enjoyable, and if we choose it, most noble — that our days work brings us these days wages in [cash?] we are not ignoble pieces of money, but comfort, and peace and happiness to our own home, help and succour to the homes of others; and the man among us who is not content, beside all this, with touching hearts and lightening lives and winning bits of light and beauty out of the world to cheer the Wayfarers, but pines to have his claps regarded as important — Man is no true brother of our toils and guild —
>
> > This I urge for teaching incidentally that Home is the scene of the first duty and the highest duty. That provision for Home is always dignified enough to make a Man. That the Greatest [domestic?] life are the duty of life, all the rest but underling [services?] — while days wages are good enough to live [illegible] [on] the [power?] of [days?] [work?]
