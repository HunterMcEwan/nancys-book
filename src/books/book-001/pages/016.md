*[The "Frank 1829 killed in battle" entry refers to **Frank Hampton**, Lt. Col. of the 2nd South Carolina Cavalry — killed at the [Battle of Brandy Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Brandy_Station) on **9 June 1863**, sabered across the face and shot in the abdomen while holding the southern section of Hansbrough Ridge against the 6th Ohio Cavalry. Brandy Station — fought near Culpeper, Virginia at the opening of the Gettysburg Campaign — was the largest predominantly-cavalry engagement of the Civil War and is widely judged the end of Confederate cavalry dominance in the East. Frank was a younger brother of [Wade Hampton III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wade_Hampton_III), the family's most famous kinsman.]*
