A handwritten sheet of genealogical notes in pencil, arranged as a family
tree with curly braces grouping each set of children. The page is faint
in places.

At the top, a paired husband-and-wife header spans the sheet:

> **Elenor Milliken** wife of —    **Thomas Milliken** — Belfast — Ireland — Merchant
>
> B - Carrickfergus, Ireland 1782          M —     **Susannah Gow** — Kirkcudbright, Scotland —
>
> D - April 21st 1844                              Died 1864 —
>
> Son —

A bracket below joins the next generation:

> { **Thomas Milliken** — Chas. —    D - 1864
>
>             M —
>
>   **Elenor Hunter** — Chas. —

A horizontal rule separates the next section. A second bracket groups
three siblings, with death-year and a marriage-and-burial annotation in
the right column:

> { **Susan Milliken**    B - Dec. 30 - 1805 — D - May 3rd - 1885
>
>   **Edmund Patrick** — B - Oct. 17 - 1810 — D - Oct. 25 - 1874
>
>   **Ellen Milliken**    B - July 25 - 1807 — D - June 10 - 1874 — M — Samuel Barker — buried Magnolia

*[The annotation "M — Samuel Barker — buried Magnolia" sits on Ellen
Milliken's row; the companion memorandum on page 222 identifies her as
the widow of S. G. Barker, buried at Magnolia Cemetery, and gives her
death year as 1871 rather than 1874.]*

A third bracket groups four brothers, with death dates written in the
right column:

> { **Adam Tunno** — B - Sept. 5th 1816 — D - Oct 31 - 1889
>
>   **John** —
>
>   **William Hunter** M. —      D - July - 20 - 1831
>
>   **Matthew Irwin** M.D. — B 1813    D - Aug 11th - 1837
>
>     died at Princeton miss.

*[The right-column death dates are written with some vertical drift; per
the companion memorandum on page 222, "D - July - 20 - 1831" is the
death of William Hunter Milliken and "D - Aug 11th - 1837" is the death
of Matthew Irwin Milliken, M.D., at Princeton, Mississippi, in his
24th year.]*

At the lower right corner, a small subtraction calculation — the
difference giving the implied birth year 1760 (consistent with Thomas
Milliken of Belfast having been 48 in 1808):

>   1808
>     48
>   ----
>   1760
