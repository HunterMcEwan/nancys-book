**Upper clipping (wedding article):**

> *The Times-News, Hendersonville, N.C., Mon., June 27, 1977*
>
> # *Miss Maria del Carmen Ochoa Kress*
> # *Weds Frank Lockwood FitzSimons III*
>
> Miss Maria del Carmen Ochoa Kress became the bride of Frank Lockwood FitzSimons III in a ceremony at St. John-in-the-Wilderness Flat Rock, at 6 p.m. Saturday, June 18. The Rev. Walter Roberts performed the ceremony. Wedding music was by William Smyth, organist and Mrs. John W. Finch, soloist.
>
> The bride is the daughter of the late Mr. Augusto Ocho and Mrs. Amelia Kress deSilvas. Her foster parents are Mr. and Mrs. Edward Gary Hartlerock formerly of Eastwood, Flat Rock, now serving as missionaries in St. John's, Antigua, West Indies.
>
> Given in marriage by her foster father, the bride wore a gown of candlelight satin and lace which she designed and made. The bodice and sleeves were of lace, with scalloped lace and pearl appliques at the hems of the sleeves and bodice. The skirt of taffeta, which fell from a crown of appliqued pearls, was bordered in lace and carried a chapel-length train. A cluster of pearl flowers and lace held her satin and pink roses from the famous Hartlerock rose gardens, her grandfather, and a lace handkerchief made from the wedding gown of her foster mother's wedding dress.
>
> Miss Susan Sapp, roommate at Middle Georgia College, Cochran, Ga., was maid of honor. Her gown was white sheer with pink dots to match her bouquet. The maid of honor's bodice was trimmed with full ruffles, and full ruffles were on the skirt of the gown. Her bouquet was of pink ribbon and flowers with pink and full length veil. She carried two long pink roses with baby's breath, nestled in lace and long pink and white ribbons.
>
> Bridesmaids were Miss Christina Mims of John's Island, S.C., cousin of the bridegroom; Miss Caroline Tatum of Beaufort, S.C.; Miss Lisa Sullivan of Florence, S.C.; Miss Mary Lou Brouwer of Henderson; and Miss Beth Conway of Hendersonville. Their dresses were identical to that of the maid of honor and each carried one long-stemmed pink rose with baby's breath, nestled in pink net with short pink and white ribbons. All the attendants' dresses were also designed and made by the bride.
>
> Miss Sara FitzSimons of John's Island, S.C., carried the traditional Guatemala wedding chain David Stinger of Hertford. The bridegroom's RR. McKinnon of Flat Rock was acolyte.
>
> The father of the bridegroom was best man.
>
> Donkin FitzSimons of Hendersonville, brother of the bridegroom, was head usher, and Walker Drabner of St. Johns Island, S.C., cousin of the bridegroom, and Robert C. Bohner of Hendersonville were the other ushers.
>
> The reception followed at the Parish House of St. John-in-the-Wilderness, where Mrs. Frank L. FitzSimons Sr., Mrs. Dalton McLEAN, Mrs. Stetson Brooking, Mrs. Gilbert Carruth, Mrs. Walter J. Roberts III, Mrs. Charles Dabner, Mrs. Hugh Endy, Mrs. Benjamin Mayhew, Mrs. Ralph Garrett, Mrs. Martin Hartlerock, Mrs. Robert Bohner and Mr. Marty Allison assisted.
>
> After the honeymoon, the couple will reside at Indigo Plantation, John's Island, S.C., where the bridegroom will be plantation manager.
>
> The bride attended private schools in Antigua, West Indies, Middle Georgia College, Cochran, Ga., and Blue Ridge Technical Institute here.
>
> The bridegroom graduated from East Henderson High School and is a 1975 graduate of Clemson University, Clemson, S.C. He served in the U.S. Navy for four years.

*[Caption beside bridal portrait:]* **MRS. FITZSIMONS**

**Lower clipping (Newberry College promotions):**

> *Newberry, S.C. Friday, Jan. 21, 1977 — THE NEWBERRY*
>
> # Four Get Promotions
> # At Newberry College
>
> Four Newberry College faculty members — Dr. John Wagner, Dr. G. F. Y. Waterhall, George Brewick, and Dwight Black — have received promotions for the 1977-76 academic year, according to President Glen E. Whitescarbo.
>
> Dr. Waterhall came to the Newberry College staff in 1965 as an instructor in English and earning his master's degree in English from the University of South Carolina. He also earned his bachelor's and his doctoral degrees from the University.
>
> In December he was named chairman of the Department of English, replacing the late Dr. Frank Stoik.
>
> Dr. Waterhall is also an associate editor of "Studies in Short Fiction," a scholarly journal published by Newberry College.
