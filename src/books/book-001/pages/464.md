> **Dear Amy:**
>
> Here are some rough notes on Stoneys that will show you something about the connection. They apparently went to Ireland from near Kettlewell in Cowan, in the West Riding of Yorkshire. They are fairly thick, and so are quite respectable, in Wexford [?] and Tipperary. "Sleatstone" is in the Dictionary of National Biography, in the Appendices. The name very apparently *[is]* Stoney-Lough = of the Stone heap — or words to that effect.
