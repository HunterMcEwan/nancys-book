*[Continued from page 500.]*

> of Philistine, what to speak not from dislike but from a desire for accuracy, one must call *vulgar*. Not coarse, naturally, but narrow in mind and heart shut in. —
>
> Ah, I wish I could take you to a quiet little village in England — a haunt of ancient peace — or to a chalet in Switzerland beneath the great walls of the mountains, the snowofields above. It seems to me we might find there a real respite and we could rewind in memory the [films] of our lives from the beginnings in old [S.C.] I could spend many hours with you talking of fathers and mothers. I dont [sic] of course regard father and mother as faultless, and I dont [sic] wish to remember them as other than they were; but if there are many people to be found of essentially finer qualities, more alive in mind and soul, of a more pretty and vital sense of the meaning of life, I want immensely to have them pointed out to me.

*[The page ends here; no signature is preserved on this sheet.]*
