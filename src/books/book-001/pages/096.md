A handwritten letter page in brown ink. The hand is small and cramped but more legible than adjacent pages. An interlinear insertion ("Mitchell") is written above the line near "Margaret's little".

> [illegible] you[r] letters to them & you let them write to you & to [save?] you additional duty—untill quite at leisure on Sunday. Your Aunty & John [walked?] to Mulbury on Thursday. The home was too cold. Is dry & yesterday have been quite warm but too cloudy & I [feel?] [will] turn cold[er] after a little rain. We have not had any thing of [Henry?] by a [steam?] last night or to day. I hope by the[n] they were busy in [Moving?]. Marian [B] is as helpless as an infant & seems [sinking?] out of [life] from her spinal disease. Frank Campbell arrived at Argyle on the 24th & Ellen is to be married on Wednesday 5th. I dont know their plans. Mr King continues to decline — but [up?] rapidly, then a Month ago. Margarets little *Mitchell* was better — had a relapse but better again since that. I will enclose [a letter?] [check?] in this letter. Tom Ferguson's Battery is at Sullivans Island. Mr Walker & his company has been sent to [some?] [illegible] engineering & Mr [Major?] [illegible] Master & them. Mr Ed White has been sent in his place but tis doubtful if he remains as he is anxious now in work at some other place. I wish these Yankees would get alarmed by Europe & go to their own country. & let us try & get our homes to be home[s]. May Capt[on?] is sick & troubled. Stills a cough & constant pain from a tooth, which unnerves him. It affects the middle of his [ear?]. They have taken an unfurnished house in Winnsboro'. Boarding being too expensive. May Capt[on?] May wish to go away — but the threat of the Sheet makes Mr [E?] think of going away. May is woken up [sic?]
