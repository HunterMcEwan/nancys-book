The page combines six mounted photographs of three generations of FitzSimonses with a dense, central column of cursive-ink genealogy in the compiler's hand. Above the genealogy is a small newspaper-clipping fragment about Hazel Duke FitzSimons of Columbia.

## Top — heading of the genealogical chart (cursive ink)

> **Christopher Fitz Simons** 4th — Born Jan 26th 1856 / Died Oct 8th 1925
>     — M. Feb 12th 1890 —
> **Frances Motte Huger.** B. Jan 10th 1863. D. Nov 11th 1937

*[The "4th" notation above "Christopher Fitz Simons" identifies this as the fourth-generation Christopher, viz. **Christopher FitzSimons Jr.** "Kit", son of Dr. Christopher FitzSimons (3rd). Family tradition counted: (1) the emigrant Christopher b. 1762, (2) his son Christopher b. ~1789, (3) Dr. Christopher b. 1826, (4) Christopher "Kit" b. 1856.]*

## Centre column — small mounted clipping fragment (newsprint glued to page)

> Although not taking the top honor at Kingstree High School, Hazel,
> now Mrs. Christopher Fitzsimons of Columbia was an honor graduate
> and went on to the University of South Carolina to become the
> second member of the family named to Who's Who. She was also
> elected to Alpha Kappa Gamma, national leadership society.

*[The clipping is undated; it post-dates Christopher F.S. (6th)'s second marriage to Hazel Duke. "Mrs. Christopher Fitzsimons of Columbia" in the late-mid-twentieth century is Hazel Duke FitzSimons, second wife of the Gen-6 Christopher.]*
