The continuation and conclusion of the poem from the previous page.

> The moon seems to shine as brightly as then,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That night when the love yet unspoken
>
> Leaped up to his lips, and when low murmured vows,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Were pledged, to be ever unbroken;
>
> Then drawing his sleeve roughly over his eyes,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He dashes off tears that are welling
>
> And gathers his gun close up to its place,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if to keep down the heart swelling.
>
> He passes the fountain, the blasted pine tree,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The footstep is lagging and weary
>
> Yet onward he goes through the broad belt of light,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Towards the shades of a forest so dreary.
>
> Hark! Was it the night wind that rustled the leaves?
>
> Was it the moonlight so wondrously flashing?
>
> It looked like a rifle — "Ha! — Mary, good bye!"
>
> ~~And the~~ life blood is ebbing and splashing.
>
> All quiet along the Potomac tonight,
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No sound save the rush of the river;
>
> While soft falls the dew on the face of the dead —
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The picket's off duty forever —
