The second sheet of the letter begun on page 358; cream wove paper, blue cursive, no salutation.

> tell Amy, but cause to all said she received every one to leave [a] nice much used. She wrote Maggie yesterday, and you might let her read this too, as I think they should know it all. She says as long as Mary Lou is this well she can manage. Amy goes to Sally Sat. — and Tuesday she plans to go every A.M. right after breakfast and stay till time to go to X-Ray. She doesn't want Marlee to know and I think would rather not any one for the present but family. I think she doesn't feel up to having to discuss it with the public, and I can see how she feels.
