notes: "Three envelopes mounted on a mostly-blank album page. Upper left: a business envelope of Hammond, Kennedy & Yow, Lawyers, Southern Finance Building, Augusta, Georgia, addressed to Miss Ellen FitzSimons care of F.L. FitzSimons at Hendersonville, N.C. (an earlier 'Charleston Library, Charleston, S.C.' address is struck through). A pencilled note above the address explains the contents: a letter from Judge Hammond about attending Ellen's mock 'funeral.' This envelope evidently held the long humorous letter transcribed on pages 537–542. Upper right: a stamped Hendersonville, N.C. envelope (3-cent Statue of Liberty stamp, postmarked 1942) addressed to Mrs. J. Pickens Walker at 3657 Richmond Street, Jacksonville, Fla. Lower right: a tilted envelope postmarked New York, Oct 8 1955, addressed to Mrs. J.P. Walker, P.O. **Upper-left envelope** — printed return address in the upper left corner:

> HAMMOND, KENNEDY & YOW
> LAWYERS
> SOUTHERN FINANCE BUILDING
> AUGUSTA, GEORGIA

Pencilled note across the upper portion of the envelope, in the compiler's hand:

> Letter from Judge Hammond telling Ellie [Walker] he had been to her funeral!

Addressed in ink:

> Miss Ellen FitzSimons,
> ~~Charleston Library,~~
> ~~Charleston, S. C.~~
> Care of F. L. FitzSimons
> Box 167   Hendersonville, N.C.

Charleston, S.C. (or Augusta, Ga.) postmark visible at the upper right.

**Upper-right envelope** — a pale prestamped envelope with a 3-cent purple Washington stamp; Hendersonville, N.C. postmark dated 1942. Return-address line printed at the top:

> After 5 days, return to
> [hand-written, partly faded: M. FitzSimons]
> Box 167
> Hendersonville, N.C.

Addressed in blue ink:

> Mrs. J. Pickens Walker,
> 3657 Richmond Street,
> Jacksonville, Fla.

**Lower-right envelope** — a small white envelope tilted on the page, New York postmark dated Oct 8, 1955. A fragment of writing in the same hand peeks out from beneath the upper edge of the envelope ("…o who a / r close / out"). Addressed in blue ink:

> Mrs. J. P. Walker
> P. O. Box 167
> Hendersonville
> N. C.

*[The three envelopes appear to be saved together because each carried family correspondence written to or about Ellen Mikell FitzSimons or her sister-in-law Amy. The Hammond letter envelope is the cover for the long jocular condolence-letter that follows on pages 537–542.]*
