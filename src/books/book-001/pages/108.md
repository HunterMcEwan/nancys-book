A handwritten letter, page 4 of multiple, in the same hand and dark ink as the preceding scans. The page is fully written top to bottom in the same dense cursive. The bottom of the sheet ends mid-sentence; the letter evidently continues onto a further leaf not in this scan run.

> *[Continuation from previous page.]*
>
> all right, still safe & well — Uncle E. says *"he fears from reports of Mr. Connie that the hot weather will not permit any conservative surgery & that amputation must ensue"* Frank Pinckney [uncertain] 1st Lieut, below by Fred Prioleau [uncertain] — Toby says I am well & feel strangely impressed with any preservation from the dangers which have *[caret: ^encircled the]* career of so many noble men of our side — We are 70, 15 miles from Richmond & close in camp — Col. W. reported for service on 25th but so common and was assigned him till 28th — he was the Infantry battalion with 3 Va. Regt. temporarily — We hear that Gabriel Hayne [uncertain] died of wounds received during the Battle. So far no news of any injury to any of our many friends in Va here come to us — Uncle Sanford [uncertain] wrote me he would like to come up here, but I rather think professional calls & meanness about the attack on Charleston will prevent — Fanny was better. & Father had an attack like the one last spring but no better. W— says on his letter "Tell Kate, Uncle John goes to bed every night with the clothes brush, on account of Red Bugs — they are a finest animal about half the size of a flea." So[uth?] Aunt Bob says Aunt Sally got a letter from Sam, 21st July, 30 miles by below Vicksburg with 7 [oz?] of our guns. The fleet above lies in sight — He is four miles from the river out of reach of shells — weather intensely hot, no rain for weeks. You hear news so fully, that I feel all the time, as if you had heard already, what I have to say, but as you, when for will own mind I write it — Father takes Richmond Whig & W— & Aunt Bob send Cha[rleston] papers & we generally see a Col's paper so that by degrees we hear of what was happened but it is all *History*, when we hear it, it has long ceased to be news at this time — I have all the time a feeling as of I was holding my breath till something is over, & as if there is not time to think or talk.
