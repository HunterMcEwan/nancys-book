An open-book scan showing two facing leaves of a handwritten letter in brown ink on lined paper. Both columns are full of dense cursive; both begin and end mid-sentence.

## Left column

> Nell & being with Auntie & next to Mother. Uncle John writes & looks forward to our coming up to, & it will give him pleasure out here, & when we are ever to get up there, is now a knotty question — any how we have it to think about — I am still wearing the bonnet you made for me just because I like it — My veil is at the back & I wear a hidden veil over my face. Bess, is one thing I have rested from there it is much to think about that cant be helped — it is a relief to get into one or two things that we just do & go along in. — William comes by and says "Tell Mary I say

## Right column

> *"Tirra-la-la-leera"* & he sings & is in a happy frame of mind, gets along finely with Louisa upsets a large King expression & sentiment to her amusement & amusement — frequently I have written a wretched scraggily letter & it will trouble you to find out the pages but it is too stupid to write over again — Harriett Crafts came in a silk dress train & looked very nicely & was so affectionate. *"Kiss Sis the the dear little Cate"* for us & never mind telling any thing they say. It is nice for having them in our laps
