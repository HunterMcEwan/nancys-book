A handwritten letter, written in cursive ink across two facing leaves of the same sheet. The text continues from the previous page and runs onto the next.

> him I was not sure where it was but I would look in Grandmother's wardrobe for it — He wanted a moment & then said *"Aunt Ellen is the [neatest?] one in there still?"* I said yes, & I would always try to keep it so — he added *"Aunt Ellen she is the [uncertain] (only) one we could trust any thing to" —* He did not know all the meaning of what he said — We will all miss the assurance of her love when trouble or joy comes to us, but when I look at Toby, & Mrs I feel as if men have a harder time than women — Wm will miss the constant delight of his life in writing to Mother & thinking of her — & Toby — I dont know how he will get on — his temperament needed so the sort of trust we could have in Mother's patient understanding sympathy. Mrs Young came to see us & in the way she talked about her grand children she spoke of Mother, [and?] she thought there could not have been a more blameless life — & how last time she had been here after little Tom's death, she had noticed the bluish [tint?] that came & went on her face like a [veil?] — Mr. Henry Bowdoin came one day himself to ask about Mother & brought in his hand 3 bottles of old wine 2 of them some of [Grand]father's which Uncle Eddie had given him after Uncle Eddie had been staying with Mrs Bowdoin [in Conwa?] some time during the war — You will be surprised to hear of Mrs Charles Lowndes' sudden death — She has been out of health for some time but they were all ready to go to the French Broad, when she woke one morning feeling badly but was dressing, & at last told Mrs Lowndes
