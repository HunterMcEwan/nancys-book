A single sheet of lined paper closely written in faded brown ink, in cursive.

> July 17th. Grandmother seemed so weak & ill that she thought herself dying — & indeed seemed to the physicians and all around to be near her end. much disabled by swelling throat not suffering save from spells of coughing & extreme emaciation. She had taken leave of some of the family the night before — kissing them — to my Aunt Ellen who has been especially despondent over her she said, "My child, I leave you in stronger arms than mine." "God help you! it is "an hard & troublesome world." — Her whole manner was serene, & untroubled by the distress of those to whom she spoke, as if she were already tasting of the peace of Heaven which opened like the "a bright vision" before her eyes — "God bless you, my children." "God help you "when I am gone." — "Remember oh! remember "this hour." "We all come to this, it only "depends on ourselves how we meet it" — these sentences uttered at intervals but in a clear distinct voice with pauses between the words to regain her breath which made them the more impressive. She called for each [grandchild?] and asked for those not near her and being especially anxious that Father might come to her — Theodore told her that he would go for him and asked if she had any message. *[the illegible]* [uncertain]
