**Featured article (center column):**

> # Frank L. FitzSimons Sr. Named Winner of Annual Wolfe Award
>
> Frank L. FitzSimons Sr., Henderson County historian, co-teacher, banker, former regular of dollar, and an avid sports devotee, has been named winner of the 1977 Thomas Wolfe Memorial Award of Western North Carolina Historical Association.
>
> The award is to be presented today at the association's quarterly meeting at the Montgomery United Methodist Church near Candler.
>
> With Andrews of Hendersonville will make the presentation, with another member of the association will then present the award.
>
> FitzSimons was chosen for his remark, "Along the Banks of the Oklawaha," a compendium of historical anecdotes about the people of Henderson County. He earned local accolades for the work, which is featured by Mr. Wolfe in writing.
>
> The Wolfe Award, a Wolfe-engraved cup, is presented annually to the Western North Carolina writer who has done the best book, in the opinion of the Wolfe Memorial Association. The award is named after the man who, perhaps to support the writing of "Look Homeward, Angel," cited Asheville as the basis of his "Old Catawba" novels.
>
> Among winners have been Glenn Tucker, who wrote articles and memoirs of the Hayward family; historians Frank Hickerson and Joseph Maddox of the second on the State and county presenter; Wilma Dykeman, author of "The French Broad"; and John Parris, for *[continues]*
