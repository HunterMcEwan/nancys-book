The typescript is paginated '58' in pencil at upper right (the probate-court page-number identifier — see pencil note 'Probate Court / Charleston SC / vol. 28, p. 58'). Typewritten reference codes '14-B' (with superscript 'S') and 'N-25.' (with superscript 'O') head the page — standard probate-office filing codes. The will gives Ann Perry the entire estate absolutely; confirms a December 1793 marriage settlement between testator, his father Edward Perry the Elder, and Rebecca Drayton; appoints Ann as Executrix and Edward Perry Senior and Tobias Bowles as Executors. Witnessed by Isaac Auld, John Mackey, and Rebecca Drayton. Proved before Charles Lining, Esq., O.C.T.D., 3 December 1800. Certified 'A True Copy' by Virginia L. > *[Pencil at upper right:]* Probate Court / Charleston SC / vol. 28, p. 58 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 58

> **WILL OF**
>
> **EDWARD PERRY, JUNIOR**
>
> 14-B<sup>S</sup>.) South Carolina.
>
> N-25.<sup>O</sup>) In the Name of God Amen, I **Edward Perry** the younger of **Saint Pauls** in the State aforesaid Planter do make and publish this my last Will and Testament hereby revoking all former Wills by me heretofore made, first it is my Will and desire that all my just debts and funeral expences be paid. Item I give devise and bequeath unto my dear and beloved Wife **Ann Perry** all my real and personal Estate of what nature or kindsoever or wheresoever unto her, her heirs, Executors, Administrators and Afsigns absolutely and for ever. Item It is my Will and desire that every Article Clause or Limitation in a certain deed or Marriage settlement bearing date the &nbsp;&nbsp; day of December 1793 duly made and executed between myself and **Edward Perry** the elder of the one part, and **Rebecca Drayton** of the other part should be fully complied with And I do by this my last Will and Testament confirm the said Deed or Marriage Settlement and every part of it. Lastly I do hereby nominate, constitute and appoint my beloved Wife Executrix and **Edward Perry** Senior, and **Tobias Bowles** Executors of this my last Will and Testament. In Witnefs whereof I have hereunto set my Hand and Seal this eighth day of June in the Year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety seven.
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; **Edward Perry, Junior** (LS)
>
> Signed, Sealed, published pronounced and declared by the Testator to be his last Will and Testament in the presence of us, who in his presence, and in the presence of each other have hereunto subscribed our Names.
>
> **Isaac Auld** ————— **John Mackey** ———————— **Rebecca Drayton**
>
> Proved before **Charles Lining** Esquire O.C.T.D. December 3, 1800
>
> At same time qualified **Ann Perry** Executrix
>
> Examined 3 Co.Sh.<sup>9</sup>) C.L.
>
> Recorded in Will Book D-1800-1807 &nbsp;&nbsp; Page 57.

> *[Stamp/ink certification at lower right:]* Attest: A True Copy &nbsp;&nbsp; \[signed] **Virginia L. McLeod** &nbsp;&nbsp; Clerk Probate Court, Charleston County, South Carolina

*[Editorial note: this is the first known will in the Perry chain reconstructed across pp. 609, 612, 610, etc. Edward Perry the Younger here is the husband of Ann (Drayton) Perry whose own 1830 will appears on p. 612; their son **Edward Drayton Perry** is named in his mother's will. The 1793 marriage settlement referenced is between testator, his father Edward Perry the Elder, and Rebecca Drayton (Ann's mother) — confirming the Drayton match. The witness Isaac Auld is likely Dr. Isaac Auld (1770-1819), Lowcountry physician; Tobias Bowles was a Charleston attorney and executor familiar in St. Pauls Parish; Charles Lining (Ordinary, Charleston Town District) signed many probates of the period.]*
