A scrapbook page given over to the Theodore Stoney FitzSimons family. Photographs are captioned in pencil and blue ink in the compiler's hand.

**Upper left — cabinet portrait of a young man with dark hair and small moustache, three-quarter view in dark coat:**

> Theodore Stoney Fitz Simons

**Upper centre — a small printed/handwritten biographical card glued at centre top:**

> Theodore Stoney Fitz Simons
> b. Jan 14th 1855 &nbsp;&nbsp; d. June 18 1900
> — M —
> Sabina MacCrady
> b. [1858]
>
> John McCready Fitz Simons — b. [Jan. 8] 1890 — d. [July 4] 1957
> Louisa De Bernière Fitz Simons — b. June 2, 1891
> Theodore Lynch Fitz Simons — b. 1892, d. Jan 31, 1956
> d. Jan 21, 1936

**Upper right — cabinet portrait of a young woman in dark dress with high lace collar, three-quarter view, hair piled up:**

> Sabina MacCrady
> wife of
> Theodore Stoney Fitz Simons

**Upper right margin — a clipped sheet of cream paper, written in ink, glued at the top corner:**

> **Last Voyage**
>
> My past is laying at my long lasts.
> Yet I cannot leave the room.
> The whites within the [veil illegible]
> Gold-rolen are the [moon].
>
> Keeping the [pilot? — partly illegible — line 1]
> Beside the [Mary — illegible word] sky
> My darkening sail — all souring still
> To dream a-found unawares —
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; *Theodore S. Fitz Simons*

*[The poem text is faded and partly illegible; the attribution at lower right confirms authorship.]*
