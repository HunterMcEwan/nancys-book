> *[Several lines at the top of the page are cross-written and largely illegible. The visible thread picks up:]*
>
> [...] my sister friend
> [I have?] to tell you of the sorrow that [has befallen?]
> all of us, & try to prepare you for the [blow?] —
> but it will come as heavily first as [it] [is?] [that] God only
> [can?] [help?] you & comfort you as it is He who
> has taken from you your dear Sister.
>
> She died this morning July 29 at the Island.
> Mrs Walker & Auntie were with her during her
> illness, & ministered to the last. She had not
> been well for some weeks before, suffering from the
> same nervous headaches she was accustomed to have.
> I saw her several times before she left town, &
> she seemed to feel depressed, & even spoke doubt-
> fully of her visit to Aunt Kate. I tried to cheer her
> up, & did somewhat. She went to the Island & there
> over-exerted herself in fixing the house for the children
> while she should be absent. Last Saturday the 27[th]
> she felt badly but able to give Mrs Bone lunch and
> chat in her usual way, but not 10 minutes after [lunch?] [...]
