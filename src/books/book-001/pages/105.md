> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Wednesday July 7th 1862
>
> Dear [Pa],
>
> Although I am sure you will have heard it, I will mention that we got Telegrams from Toby of 27, 31, & later & a Richmond despatch of 1st [uncertain] containing the accounts of the Battle up to that time. By this time the news got to us here. It is so old that it seems useless to send it to you. Since the 31st we have no further news from Toby. The mail comes tonight & if any thing comes from him I will copy it. The Telegrams are safe & will [uncertain] safe & will — glorious news 28th — There has been literally nothing to tell you about, since Mother wrote, or one of us would have written before. If you could spend a week here you would realize what active citizens are to us. I have never been on a plain anything like it before & I constantly imagine what our feelings will be when we get into the old leaky [chamber?] with a hole in the top shaky boards under your feet & two or three whole glasses out of the window that should be there. I mean how happy we will be we constrast to our feeling the night we got here. But I really feel very thankful for being here in a great many respects, & if it were not for the awful anxieties of the times the mail arrangements would not seem so hard to bear patiently. Now & then some one coming direct from Charlotte & more often from Columbia brings a paper only, a day old & it is handed around the hotels & sometimes part of the village at a treasure. Upon Allen & those from Greenville come on these days. Gone Vallop, the first in every little scrap that you can about the children like Christie me all would be too glad to come to your nice country but it is so far. I am afraid we cant get there. Tell him he must try & remember how me look, I wish I had him here now. This thin ink is bad to. Mother is sewing out on the piazza. Kate has gone
