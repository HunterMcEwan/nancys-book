notes: "Typewritten letter dated September 12th, 1918, addressed to 'Mr Thomas E. Osborne, Horse Shoe, N.C.' from W. Huger FitzSimons. Conveys the terms on which his sister (Ellen B. FitzSimons) is willing to sell her Mills River Farm — referring back to her letter of January 16th, 1917: fifteen thousand five hundred ($15,500.00) dollars for the Farm, not including the Mountain land which she wishes to reserve; $5000.00 cash and balance ($10,500.00) in five equal successive annual installments of $2100.00 each with interest on all unpaid credit portion at six (6%) per cent per annum payable annually; advises Mr Osborne to wait for sister's confirmation but expects to get the rent for this year. He had written to Santor (or Sauter) from Hendersonville and explains that he had promised to mention Mr Osborne and would shortly be in Charleston. Signed 'Sincerely yours,' (typed name not visible). Below the typescript a handwritten note in blue ink reads: 'Dear Sister: / Above is a copy of my letter / to Mr Osborne of this date. / [initial flourish — possibly 'H.J.G.' or similar] / Sept 12/18.' Several details in the typewritten body are uncertain because of strikeover and a faded ribbon."

> September 12th, 1918
>
> Mr Thomas E. Osborne,
> Horse Shoe,
> N.C.
>
> Dear Mr Osborne:—
>
> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I have had a talk with Sister and she is willing
> to sell you her Mills River Farm upon the same terms as mention-
> ed in her letter to you of January 16th, 1917 viz: Fifteen thous-
> and five hundred ($15,500.00) Dollars for the Farm, not including
> the Mountain land; she wishes to reserve the Mountain land.
> Terms $5000.00 cash and the balance ($10,500.00) by purchase money
> notes and mortgage, payable in five equal successive annual in-
> stalments of $2100.00 each with interest on all unpaid credit
> portion at the rate of six (6%) per cent per annum payable annually
> Please advise if this will be satisfactory to you. Sister says
> if you should buy at these terms she would of course still ex-
> pect to get the rent for this year. I wrote to Santor from Hen-
> dersonville as I promised you but mentioned to her that I would
> shortly be in Charleston and she answered by saying that she
> would look up the former letter she had written you and she con-
> cluded to wait until I could come to Charleston to talk the mat-
> ter over with her and that is the reason I have not written to
> you sooner. With kind regards,
>
> Sincerely yours,

*[Handwritten note in blue ink at lower left:]*

> Dear Sister:
>
> Above is a copy of my letter
> to Mr Osborne of this date.
>
> [initialed flourish, possibly 'H.J.G.']
>
> Sept 12/18.
