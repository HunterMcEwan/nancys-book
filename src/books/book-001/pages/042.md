A handwritten letter in brown ink on a single page, closely written. Annotations in pencil at the foot identify the principal figures.

> the state of things will not last much longer but that an [Mr. Petty] will be some [comfort to him]. He is so [chest] as you are shouts to be tally to a [staple]. Henry, Aunt little Ellen [&] Pa has been a real comfort & Pleasure on the [F. S.]. Sis Theo & Sis Ed Bertha what I have now in [Patrick]. God can not grant such things, but I think she is one of the most well [behaved] children I ever knew to do with everything of her. Aunt Hetty has to work, has she gone to [Wraithfield]. Cleve [Maly] she is a most little creature how often I have through how happy her dear papa would be if he had been spared to see his youth past her morn. — I must say Mr. Hetty much was able to about this children of [Jamie's] for [winter] than I did before her. To much, but I see she is here. Mary [a particular] [allow on to dry] you have my [admiration] for the manner in which you so [bravely] the dear [letter] and the boys are uncommonly good children. May they not be spared to you & advise your devotion to them with [much] respect to children — I regret much to hear Ellen but [Aunt] [Hetty] has been able to [ask me to see her]. We had a [visit] from [Andrew] is do so. He leaves [days till] [were] sent them better off [for] Mr. Tomeny, & I shall endeavor to go I will see [letting] on Thursday. John says you must not be ready about [Ellen]. She has [taken] [your] one of two and I do so. He stays much with the children to a great pet with Pa. He let her in [day] of his sister in lots [Pinerc to stay with] Julia Yeamans. She instantly explains the [Goss] she says she likes by his sister no fact she wants to see. [Mary Ann] the boys go [in expectation] [now] to [Mat's bay] on [Friday]. Pa & [Linnie] will dine [her] there. — I think [Tem] go to [Black] her next Saturday for a few days I [return there]. I have [not] [Mr. Hire] yet so how I will go to Charleston, but I think about the [13th] or [14th] of [next] [Month]. I wish to stay in C[harleston] [10] days then [return to] Georgetown. — I just back not having so [seen] him here at [F. S.] [&] [day with] is a great [pleasure] to be without the Church Service & I have been [neglecting it all day]. — Sis [Mary] to hear Eugenia is not going to [Bance] Cousin May the Children [will be a pain or a change]. Cassy my love to him I have written him so a few days. — With much for the boys & yourself. I am as ever yours [affec:] mother
>
> C. P. FitzSimons
