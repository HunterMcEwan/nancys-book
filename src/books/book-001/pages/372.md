Second sheet of Sam's letter to his mother, continuing in blue-ink cursive.

> we left that we would do it after much discussion and thought. What it amounts to is this. I'll work for him one year and after that if I'm satisfied with the work and he's satisfied with me I'll start *[buying]* him out and this may take from 10 to 20 years. He made it very clear to us that it would all be business and there would be nothing for free. In fact he came out and told me that he couldn't give it to us since Janice has a sister & who is *[also]* entitled to just as much of it as we are. This is the only way I would agree to working for him now. The salary isn't as much as I'm making now, come July I'll be making $10,000 a year, but I'm sure both of us will be a lot happier living in Panama City, knowing that we don't stand any chance of being transferred. I told them out at the mill yesterday that I would be leaving in three weeks and they sure were real nice *[continues on page 373]*
