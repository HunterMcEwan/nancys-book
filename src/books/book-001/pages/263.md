notes: "Continuation of the multi-page manuscript letter from pages 261–262. Written in brown ink in a more open hand than 262 (largely horizontal, not cross-hatched), making this page substantially more legible. The text is a deathbed scene: the dying woman tells Mrs. Northrop that she is willing to die and wishes to be baptised by the Priest; Mrs. Northrop and the Priest succeed in clearing the room; the dying woman declares 'I do it only on his account I am unchanged and die in the faith of my forefathers and you must say so'; Mr. Walker objects to her baptism but she takes his hand and turns her wasted face to him, saying 'I say it is only right to Christian as he thought I could not go to Heaven unless Baptised but it is only to make him happy'. The writer reports a long conversation with Mrs. Walker about being a Protestant, his trust in God's mercy for pardon, and concern for the children: 'Yes tell Mother that I have thought of this continually since she left & throughout my illness with intense affection; tell her that I thought of her in my dying moments, gave her my love.'
