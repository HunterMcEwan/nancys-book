An open-book scan showing two facing leaves of a handwritten letter in brown ink on lined paper. The left leaf is paginated **4** at the top centre and is the continuation of the previous letter; the right leaf opens a fresh letter dated **Charleston / Thursday** *for* (?) and addressed **My dear Mary**.

## Left column (page 4 — continuation)

> under Dr. Forrest — To return to the "original Evening." After hats had been taken off, they returned to the dining Room to tea, where Mr. McMillan & Miss Susie came in, then Mrs. Williman & Susie. So then was quite a company down at the table — After tea they went up Stairs to see the drawing Room, & while there, Mary & Hennie & Mr. Bryan. Mr. Hugh Maynes & Arthur came So there was a lot of [people sitting] & talking going on —
>
> they all went, & we returned to the dining Room, where We Smoked, & Louisa Sat by him & they looked married — He was Expected about Ten o'Clock on Sunday, when King came

## Right column — new letter

> Charleston
>
> Thursday for [?]
>
> My dear Mary
>
> Your dear lovely letter came today & thanks for it. Nell & I were very happy over it. We had meant to write So you could have letters at the time of all the things going on, but we were too busy — well, they went to Mulberry & had a very happy time. fancy this, they never were down until near Eleven o'Clock to breakfast. Can you imagine Nell & I to be So reckless of proprieties?! Of course they had the meal all to themselves at that hour
