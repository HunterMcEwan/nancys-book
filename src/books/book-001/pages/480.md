A single sheet of US Army stationery, printed at the top:

> AMERICAN EXPEDITIONARY FORCES
> HEADQUARTERS SERVICES OF SUPPLY
> TRANSPORTATION SERVICE

Datelined at the upper right in cursive ink:

> A.P.O. 717, France.
> August 10, 1918.

> "My dear Mr. Fitz —
>
> Just a line to tell you that I have seen your boy — *"Our aviator"*. While having dinner one [day] in the principal Street of this city, I suddenly saw a very sweet face that seemed familiar to me. I soon realized that both of us knew. He had seen the other, [some]where. But I passed on and so did he. We are on opposite sides of the Street. In a few minutes I felt a hand on my arm and heard the question: *"Don't you remember Major B—?"* Of course I could not deny it and I knew him across at once. The last time we'd met was in the New Willard Hotel when I [almost] had a fight with the man who wanted our chair. Do you remember it?
>
> The boy looks fine and has all the pride and enthusiasm and courage and hope of the best of South Carolinians & other days. I was very proud of him and took him to see the French people who take care of me and they fell in love with him at once. We had dinner together and the following afternoon I went to the aviation field [continued on next page]…"

*[The letter breaks off at the foot of the page; the continuation is not present in the album. The writer's signature is not visible; the addressee 'Mr. Fitz —' is almost certainly W. Huger FitzSimons, and 'Our aviator' his son Gavin FitzSimons, who served as a U.S. Army pilot in France in 1918.]*
