notes: "First page of a three-page letter (continues on pages 493 and 494) written from Adams Run, S.C., October 12, 1905, by Minnie P. FitzSimons to 'My dear Aunt Louisa.' Written in dark ink in a sloping cursive on a single sheet, head-down. Minnie has just read a letter from 'Uncle Theodore' (presumably Theodore G. Barker, whose letter to Ellen at Spartanburg is mounted on page 491) addressed to 'Sam' — her husband or relative Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons — and is shaken by news of the imminent death of a beloved elderly woman who has been 'a Mother to me since I came among you.' The corresponding envelope, addressed to Miss Ellen M. FitzSimons in care of the Charleston Library (struck through) and re-directed to 131 Tradd Street, Charleston, is mounted on page 488 — the letter was apparently delivered to Ellen at the Tradd Street address where Aunt Louisa was living."

> Adams Run, S.C.
> October 12th, 1905.
>
> My dear Aunt Louisa,
>
>           I have just read Uncle Theodore's letter to Sam, and of course its contents is a grief and shock; I have often told myself that she is old and not strong, and yet she kept such a brave front and was so much to us all that I could never bear to think of her going.
