A composite album page with four pasted items.

**Upper left — newspaper front page:**

The masthead reads:

> The Corner Cupboard
> AN INDEPENDENT NEWSPAPER
> Orlando, Florida, November 14, 1957
> Volume Eight Number 52 — Price 10 Cents A Copy

A small inscription at the top of the masthead in pencil:

> Mrs. J. P. Walker Jr.
> 4275 [illegible] Ave.

The lower half of the clipping is mounted inverted; visible elements include the word "**SALE!**" running upside-down, the price "$2.60," and a photograph (upside-down on the page) of a man at a counter — apparently a retail advertisement. A column of body text at the lower right runs:

> …reform they will Jim is one of the kind whom he hunts on the [illegible] week for the [illegible] Friday is…

**Upper right — black-and-white snapshot:**

Three people seated very close together on what looks like a sofa or chair: at the left an older grey-haired man in a light open-collar shirt smiles toward the others; at the center a dark-haired woman in a short-sleeved patterned dress looks affectionately at the young woman on the right, who wears a light short-sleeved dress with a wide collar and has dark wavy shoulder-length hair. No caption.
