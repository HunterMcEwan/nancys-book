> About sixty-five years ago, a homesick young man in his late twenties, walked between his house and his drydocks store in Montgomery, Alabama. Twice a day he passed a mill that crushed cottonseed, making cake and meal and a small black, sticky oil that consumed cotton wool. The young man never paused (he plead when it was not in operation) and a vision gradually came to him. With his tremendous opportunity and the gift of seeing — it would lead in all in his store thinking that someday this oil might be useful "for many purposes"?
