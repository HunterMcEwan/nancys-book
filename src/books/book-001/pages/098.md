> [the same?] subject. Will got letters on Monday from

> Frank was better, but he had been

> very anxiously & there were three Physicians attending.
> He was most ill the day they
> thought the city might be Shelled & it would have been
> impossible to move him. They

> try & escape from N. O. & come to No. Ca. The letter Ellen

> who escaped — & taken to Flat Rock by young Arthur
> Huger, who had been taken in the [Peninsula?] &
> confined in Phila. Cecelia Campbell had written

*[The letter's geography traces a wartime communication network: a Confederate officer captured in the [Peninsula Campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsula_campaign) and held in Philadelphia carrying a New Orleans letter overland to Flat Rock, NC — the Henderson County village known as the "Little Charleston of the Mountains" and a principal refuge for Lowcountry families during the Civil War. "Brain fever" was a period catch-all for high fevers with delirium, often typhus, typhoid, or meningitis.]*
