A single sheet of lined paper closely written in faded brown ink, in cursive. The lower portion of the sheet is blank. Red staining is visible along the lower edge.

> she listened — and when asked if she had heard said yes — seeming to receive comfort —
>
> Sunday 8th. Grand Mother still living, without taking nourishment sufficient apparently to keep up life, in a stranger frame — never complaining but suffering a great deal, last night thought dying, this morning revived & quite collected, knowing every one about her! Mr Drayton I am told had ~~not~~ interpreted this prolonged life of suffering as God's way of showing the perfecting [power?] of His grace "In your patience possess ye your souls", a more powerful manifestation of [this for?] religion than volumes of sermons — praying that it might not be in vain to those who were [its] witnesses.
