Continuation of the letter from page 061. Same hand, same brown ink, on a second leaf.

> Paul. The latter still on visited & on her chamb[er] for the last few weeks I look very poorly. & a baby little well. she a very large baby. Yesterday looked exactly like [Sister?]. So [tonight?] [she] do both [those?] friends. *Sue* & *himself* I will assure he feels you would not have gone to see him the day you went to town. I have told him from him since you left, you knew the whole [note] go to Mulberry with the girl. there now is much sickness amongst the negros [or] [wagons]. We all & Soso, not nearly. I do hear last Saturday from [Bell] date 13 Jan. I left a bundle in town for E. M. B. to forward, fearing to send the chicken clothes to Mr B. He did inform me with you (which you Father shipment for you) you found him the [folks?] & I thought they ought to be [bro?] [out?] of [Mumter?]. Poor thumble was also lost at the same time. Kate said you before you went [out?] [returned] in town, or of they on enquired you on it to her. They said as at Mary & on Tuesday & got into the carriage with a 4 [tete?] [Note?] he & I Hagar were intended to bring up to Bottom the next day & Alabama girls. The waited a [vile?] [solitation?]. & so I had taken the last cut of my bushes that day & mountain of your Father had ordered it to be [unflinishéd?] [as?] doors to West [Fuller?] got a small key of [Mutton?] & a [refrain?]. The Sue & Champagne [Skirt?] & left them forgetting to take to ship at the [Backers?]. but [I?] [refreshed?] them home in some milk. & had it but for them Kate & myself made a sponge cake nicely flavored, but not so spongy as I hoped, what was sent to E. & I [proved?] to be the spread the rapest at the Smith one of the morning some on the dinning table & I had [chad] & Wham to attend, but when one came at night I [feel?] like Mrs. Macomba drawing on the old man room boil glove to receive his guest when I had [tumbled?] down to put a [warm?] *Tom* being that day sick. Mr Burnett still came with them. the long reason to Miss Pithian intoned to Miss Foulke, & [that?] said a genial part [my?] thought she had come to make their [chere?] then alive. Mr. J. is alone — full of bustle as my pretty. Self & quite pretty, beautiful, soft black eye & good complexion more [print?] like
