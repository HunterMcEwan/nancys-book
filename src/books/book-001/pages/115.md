A handwritten letter page in brown ink, page 1 of multiple. The top of the sheet bears a one-line forwarding note above the date heading.

> [Sent?] your letter to us to the Care of Robt. McKay Esq.
>
> Greenville 14th July [63?]
>
> Dear Sir,
>
> Your letter telling us of the sickness of the Boys made a very anxious — sad — and worrying — came from Charleston on Tuesday with the good news that of their being better — & relieved that care. — I felt of them — Then knew to look at William equally [tilted?] with them — your letter last night made us idle [happier?] about them — We have no telegram or letter from there — Susie heard on [Sat?] that a telegram had come to Mr Hampton saying [Doel?] in [a way?] [recovered?] — but nothing more. — She would also state when she did again — Some of Mr A. his children were ill — a conversation but did not hear of what they were ill — We live on calmly — but very anxious — of one elder the eye & ten intended is the wish & care in my devotion to which we turn our thoughts — Joe Ferguson is ill of Typhoid fever at the Barrows — your uncle said he feared it would be a severe case — A letter from Thomas writes that "Joe is ill" & that they were not but very little see [him?] him." — The boys have written twice to us since the attack — we must each hope & pray for God's Merciful mercy is to sain & deliver suffer them — if it would be sacrificed all our brave men who must die for us — Your Aunts wrote me by Susie's terms died yesterday after eighteen days
