An open-book scan showing two leaves of handwritten letters in brown ink. The left leaf is rotated 90° (text running sideways up the page) and contains the closing of one letter; the right leaf begins a new letter with a dateline at the upper right.

## Left leaf — closing of preceding letter (text running sideways)

> with Best Expectation. I have you spend your vacation with us — Write & tell me, that you will come to us & also when your School breaks up.
>
> Yours affectionately
>
> Theodore G. Barker

> Miss Ellen M. Barker
>
> Asheville
>
> N.C.

## Right leaf — opening of new letter

> Charleston SC
>
> May 30. 1900.
>
> Dear Ellen — This is to notify you, that you must not Engage yourself to do any work during your vacation this summer — but must hold yourself free to come to Brookland, when your Aunt Louisa opens her house there — *(Don't you like the way people say "Must" to you?)* Of course, if you are offered a trip to Europe or
