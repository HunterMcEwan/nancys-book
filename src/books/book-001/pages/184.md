A typewritten sheet. An engraved coat of arms appears at the top of the page: a helm with mantling, a shield bearing a lion rampant between three (or more) ornaments, supported by lions, with a banner below reading **Barker**. A faint pencilled "**Barker**" is written to the left of the banner.

> ### BARKER COAT OF ARMS
>
> From Burke's Heraldry. Fairbairn's Crests and histories of the family.
>
> Barker: Azure a lion rampant argent within an orle of eight fleur de lis or.
>
> Crest: Issuant from a ducal coronet or, a griffin's head proper.
>
> Motto: "Fide sed cui vide." (Have confidence, but see in whom you place it.)
>
> This is one of the very distinguished old families of England whose history can be traced back to 1200 A.D. The early ancestral seat was at county Salop, England. Sir John Barker was a member of Parliament during the reigns of Charles II and James II. Other prominent members of the family in the early days were Sir William Barker, M.P. temp. Queen Anne and Sir Christopher Barker who was with Henry VIII at Calais. There have been many distinguished members of the family in America.
>
> The lion was a favorite symbol in the blazoning of arms and showed great strength and fortitude. An "orle" meant a wreath or ring of the fleur de lis (the French national flower). This showed a French origin and followers of William the Conqueror at the time of the Norman Invasion. The crown showed a connection with nobility, and the griffin (an unconquerable monster) super strength. Azure (blue) denoted truth and sincerity. Or (gold) was for stainless honor. The leaves are for decoration only and have no meaning.
