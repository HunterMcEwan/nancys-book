A handwritten letter occupies the left leaf, continuing from the previous page. A second handwritten letter begins on the right leaf, dated **Saturday June 27**. The figure **1874** is pencilled at the top right of the right leaf.

## Left leaf (continuation)

> she had so much pain in her side she would like a poultice — He instantly ran to Dr. Rougier's room & the Dr. slipped on his dressing gown & ran down but she had died before. Mr L & the Dr. got into the room — Dr. Keating Simons also died suddenly at the Burrows' — Fannie came to see us today — She is well & looks so — her baby is thin & they have to give it the bottle part of the time — She says Susie came down to see the baby & asked for Ellen as soon as she came — I want to get your work done, but with Thomas, William, & Annette here, & our friends coming constantly, there seems no time — The [Maggets?] came to see [you?] often you left, & begged me to tell you [from?] [uncertain] they had come to see you — Miss Ellen & the boys for us all. We think of you all the time —

## Right leaf (new letter)

> 1874

> Saturday June 27 [no year written]
>
> Dear Sis, I send a package of letters we have got, will you send back Toby's as soon as convenient — The others you can keep & return at any time — We have not heard from you yet, it seems longer I know than it really is, because the past week has seemed like a life time, & Ellen & you are so much in our thoughts with Mother, that it is impossible to keep down our anxieties — Toby asks each day as he comes in, if we have heard from you through the Postman or some one with letter — We send you the Telegrams daily feeling they would make you feel nearer than waiting for letters & when
