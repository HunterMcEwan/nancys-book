Explains that her grandfather was never much talked about, and that the following account of his death appeared in the Charleston Courier (the writer is uncertain whether the paper was at that time called simply 'The Courier' or whether it was the Charleston Courier and the copyist abbreviated it). Records the date of the published account as Saturday, May 19, 1866, and notes that he was killed on May 17, 1866. A pencilled cross-reference 'Note – 6.' appears at the foot. The continuation of the pencilled note in the compiler's hand. The writing fills the upper half of the sheet; the lower half is blank.

> was never any talk of him. —
>
> The following account of his death was in the Charleston Courier — I do not know if the paper was then just called the Courier — or if in copying they just put that, may — of Saturday — May 19th 1866
>
> He was killed on May 17th 1866
>
> Note – 6.
