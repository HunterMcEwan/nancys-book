An open-book scan showing two facing leaves of handwritten letters in brown ink. The left leaf is the end of a letter; the right leaf begins a new one with a dateline at the upper right.

## Left leaf — closing of preceding letter

> the [Exhilirating] & bracing sea-breeze which makes the 2nd Story piazza in Tradd Street so delightful — Ask Theodore ("Toto") if he knows anywhere a finer climate.
>
> While writing this letter I have driven off the first mosquito I have had to defend myself from this season — until it is now the 10*[th]* of June! It is now beginning to rain after a long, long Spell of dry weather, and no doubt the mosquitoes will come after the rain — but they are never very bad in Tradd Street, and to your Mother & yourself it will bring back tender reminiscences of former battles & sloppings & doings & being a *trois* of the divine song around your Ears —
>
> My Love to your Mother & Tom
>
> Yours affectionately
>
> Theodore G. Barker

> *[Pencilled annotation beside signature]:* Tom Aunt Louisa's son, [illegible] said it all, that Adam wrote.

> Miss Ellen M. Barker
>
> Asheville
>
> N.C.

## Right leaf — opening of new letter

> Charleston SC June 10 1904
>
> My dear Ellen
>
> Until your Mother's letter to your Aunt Ellen mentioned it, I did not know that your school had stopped — I had been looking forward to it and regretted that your Aunt Louisa & I will not be at Brookland until July — I would have liked to think that you and your Mother would be willing to come over and pay us a visit there — I think you both need a change, and altho' there is not much change of climate from Asheville to Hendersonville I think that it would be something for you both to see different things — Even woods & hills and rivers — than those you have been looking at for so many years without interruption — and then the going back will make it look home things look brighter — I still hope that you will be
