notes: "Typescript copy, third page of the will of Ann Drayton Perry. Page numbered '#3' at upper right; '790' written in pencil at corner. The page continues the distribution of named enslaved people among the testator's grandsons (Charles Carroll Perry, John Drayton Perry, Edward D. Perry — including the wenches Sally and Betty and the child David), then turns to debts owed to the estate, directing the executor to cancel all notes held against Edward D. Perry, and authorizes the executor to acquire a female and male servant for every female and male child of Edward D. Perry's future marriage. Transcribed literally for archival fidelity; period spelling and capitalization preserved. Enhanced re-read of the high-resolution scan resolves the grandson's full name as **Charles Carroll Perry** (phase-1 dropped the middle name); the 'Carroll' very likely commemorates Edward D. Perry's first wife Rachel Carroll (see FAMILY-NOTES Perry section)."

Item I give and bequeath to my Grand son **Charles Carroll Perry** a negro Boy named **Stephen**, to him his Executors Administrators and Afsigns forever. Item I give and bequeath to my grand son **John Drayton Perry** a negro boy named **Frederick** to him his Executors administrators and afsigns forever. Item I do hereby give and bequeath unto my son **Edward D. Perry** his Executors and Administrators my two negro wenches named **Sally** and **Betty** In trust that he and they will permit or suffer them and each of them to work out and take and receive their own wages or hire as long as they live for their own support and maintenance they respectively paying thereout their own taxes and badges and also to permit the little boy named **David** to remain with **Betty** who is his grand mother, as long as she shall live and after her death the said boy David, to sink into and become a part of the Residuum of my estate. Item I do hereby exonerate release and forever discharge my said son **Edward D. Perry** from all the debts which he owes me, and the demands I have against him whether by note Bond, Judgement, or otherwise: and I do order and direct, that all and every such note Bond or other evidence of debt of his which I hold against him be immediately upon my death cancelled and delivered up to him or taken by himself-, into his own custody to be cancelled and destroyed, and also thatfull satisfaction or satisfactions be thereupon entered up of Record upon each & every Judgement against him at any instance or suit in any of the Courts of this state and I do hereby authorise and empower my attorney or attorneys in any such cases to enter the necefsary satisfactions and discharges upon those Judgements.
