notes: |
  A printed City of Charleston Department of Health 'Return of a Birth' form, recording the birth of Amy Perry FitzSimons — the album's compiler — on 4 February 1888 at 4 Green Hill Street, Charleston. The form's hand-typed entries record her mother's maiden name (Minnie W. FitzSimons, née Perry), her father (Samuel FitzSimons, occupation 'Rice Planter'), and the attending physician (Jno. L. Ancrum). The original date filed is February 14, 1888 (No. 27-27). The document is a certified copy issued 28 January 1950: a circular embossed seal at right reads 'TRUE COPY FROM THE ORIGINAL / COUNTERSIGNED / HEALTH OFFICER / CHARLESTON CO. DEPT.', and the form bears the printed name and signed countersignature of Leon Banov, M.D., Health Officer (Banov was Charleston's long-serving Health Officer from the 1920s through the 1960s). The footnote '*name added by affid. 1-28-50' indicates the child's given name 'Amy Perry FitzSimons' was supplied by affidavit on 28 January 1950, the same date the certified copy was issued. The Charleston city seal appears at upper left bearing the legend AEDES MORES JURAQUE CURAT / CIVITATIS REGIMINE DONATA / CAROLOPOLIS / CONDITA AD / 1670.

  This is a 1950 certified copy of the 1888 birth record, not the original return. The Charleston city seal printed at upper left is the historic municipal seal of Charles Town (founded 1670). Leon Banov (1888–1971), Charleston's Health Officer for over forty years, signed in his official capacity. The footnote "name added by affid. 1-28-50" records that Amy's first name was supplied by affidavit on 28 January 1950 — evidently the original 1888 return had been filed before her parents settled on the name. The father's stated birthplace "St. John" is St. John's Berkeley, the FitzSimons parish in the South Carolina Lowcountry. The mother's birthplace "Barnwell" refers to Barnwell District, S.C., the Perry family home.
