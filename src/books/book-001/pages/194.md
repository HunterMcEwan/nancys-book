An open-book scan showing two facing leaves of a handwritten letter in brown ink on lined paper. Both columns are full of dense cursive; both begin and end mid-sentence as the page is the middle of a longer letter.

## Left column

> who had been going to Church every Sunday for so long, & sitting just there — He dined, & after a talk after dinner, he wisely left them together, & said it was time to go to Church — Mrs. Williams & Mary Lay came in *[after church]* & Mary [uncertain] [Phane] *[appeared]* into the room said "I dont mean to speak to you" whereupon she [says] "Why, yes dear, & kisses her" — So you see how gay & lively he is — Harriett Crafts came to our room after seeing "Barker" down stairs & we had a nice little time together. — We had just finished tea — which entertainment we have at table now, when Addy came in

## Right column

> & spent the evening Sol was bright for them — Monday we worked around the house [Nell] & I to get things ready for the next morning when [Annie?] [unpacked] & several of her family were here to assist her — She went round to Broad St before dinner & returned on "Brother G*[en]*" — before S*[ol]* B came home from Minnie & after it I happened into the next room when I found Sol & Susan discussing tea spoons. I broke up the party by telling Nell she ought not to be keeping Minnie in there as Theodore was waiting for her in the garden at [Sophie's]. Nell says in a temper "Now they don't stay, get [Sophie]
