A handwritten letter, page 1 of multiple, in brown ink on plain paper, in a hurried sloping cursive.

> Dear Saul
>
> yours per mail [recd] and as you have not acknowledged a letter enclosing some orders and shall probably want some money for our sale tomorrow [will] [send] some [overseer] about the boat [unless] [perchance] a few miles or without money below is a list of the orders and a [nothing?] [object?] is the balance of fillies and the heavy half of the stock boy's baggage. I shall think that the [D] would do better gave some sale silver home for, than $350 and should like [really?] to sell a few families of the purchasers without some and see them. Although not indisposed from any scruples, to our negroes, it has always been [repugnant?] to me to sell & move so to divide families.
>
> we are in a great Muck in our parish at present in these hot times, candidates aiding to and pro Bryan support and even the [former?] some of you [Charleston] tactic to cover this our inabilities. for they got the old man in & some how in the scrape and brought him up enough we rather [wills?] but I get some good by it. hearing from the Old people Mr Ben brought me a few the [even?] medal and having taken some [are?] am better and do not know as I shall obey your orders to come down as soon as expected. he
