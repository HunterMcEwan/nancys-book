A full feature article occupying most of page **6-C** of **The News and Courier, Charleston, S.C., Sunday Morning, March 26, 1961**.

## Headline

> **Gen. Wade Hampton — War and Reconstruction Hero**
>
> **South Carolina Marks His Birthday Anniversary Tuesday and Reaffirms Faith In The Principles He Upheld**
>
> By RALPHE JEROME CARNAHAN
> *Special Writer*

## Opening paragraphs

> Few of the dignified Charlestonians who [pass?] up and down [our?] gracious thoroughfares of historic Charleston, where the great Civil War October 4th, 1864, during a brilliantly executed cavalry raid against the Union forces, capture more than 2,500 head of cattle. Throughout the entire war, Hampton, with a magnetic personality, an iron will, [a?] strong sense of duty, and Christian fortitude, …

[Body of the article continues in three columns, narrating Hampton's life from his Mississippi childhood through his Confederate service, his command of the cavalry of the Army of Northern Virginia after the death of J. E. B. Stuart, the 1864 Beefsteak Raid, his postwar return to South Carolina, his role in the 1876 "Red Shirt" campaign and governorship, his service in the United States Senate, and his death. Text density at this scan resolution makes full transcription impractical.]

## Caption — engraved portrait (lower left)

A bust engraving of Hampton in Confederate uniform, full beard, three stars on his collar. Caption beneath:

> Wade Hampton As Confederate General

## Caption — photograph (lower right)

A photograph of four white columns standing in an open field. Caption beneath:

> Only Columns Remain Of Milwood, Hampton Home

[Milwood, the Hampton family seat near Columbia, was burned by Federal troops in February 1865. The article uses the surviving columns as its concluding visual metaphor for the ruin and recovery of South Carolina.]
