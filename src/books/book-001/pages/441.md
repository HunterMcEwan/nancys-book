An album page with three studio cabinet photographs arranged around a small handwritten family chart at the top centre, with a typed paper slip mounted at the lower right.

## Centre chart (handwritten in blue ink)

> **Seaman Sinkler Fitz Simons.**
> Born — — Died —
>           — M —
> **Henrietta Gaillard**
> Born — — Died Dec. 17[th] 1917
>           |
> **Christopher Fitz Simons**
> Born 1888 — Died March 24[th] 1898

## Upper-left photograph

A studio bust portrait of a bearded man with dark hair and full beard, wearing a dark three-piece suit and dark cravat. The mount is dark with cut corners. Caption in pencil beneath:

> Seaman Sinkler Fitz Simons

## Upper-right photograph

A studio portrait of a woman seated in dark Victorian dress with high collar and dark hair pulled back; a small girl in a light frilled dress stands beside her with one hand resting on the woman's lap. The mount is cream-coloured with deckled edges. Caption in pencil beneath:

> Henrietta Gaillard Fitz Simons

## Lower-centre photograph

An oval-vignetted studio portrait of a small boy with very short pale hair and a wide white collar over a dark jacket, mounted on a heavy grey-green card with the photographer's name printed in gilt at the foot of the mount (largely illegible at this scan, but appears to read "[N.S. Sham?]berg, [Charleston?], S.C."). Caption in pencil on the album beneath:

> Christopher Fitz Simons

## Lower-right slip

A small piece of folded typewriter paper has been mounted face-down on the album page; the typed text shows through faintly and reversed. Nothing on the visible side can be read with confidence.

*[The pencilled date "March 24th 1898" for Christopher's death and the inscription "died in Waycross Ga. March 27th 1898 in the 11th year of his life" on the family chart on page 271 differ by three days; both are recorded in the compiler's hand.]*
