> afternoon to call upon Mrs. Motte Pringle
> with Sue King, also Mrs. Raymond & our
> neighbour, and Mr. Barker's family from
> Mulberry — The Moultrie House has
> been refurnished & repacked, & opened on
> the 1st July by Newman who used to keep
> the Charleston, & is to open the Singleton House
> in Mt. Pleasant. I hope we shall have some
> hops — Mrs. C. & R. — want to get up [private?]
> theatricals — the School for Sunday the play
> to be performed, I am to be sad, & we will
> dare say it will, be quite amusing
> & make a variety in the sameness of
> our Island entertainments —
>
> The great feature of the season is
> a series of entertainments giving by
> Mrs. Aiken to the town in general,
> almost everyone is invited, the people
> go early or rather one or [pieces?] early
> & water about 12 o'C or a little after —
>
> The amusement consists of a good
> band of music, they play overtures
> Quadrilles, Polkas & & & & the company
> dance or not as they wish — there is
