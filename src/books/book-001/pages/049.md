A single sheet of lined paper closely written in faded brown ink, in cursive. A few short marginal marks (crosses) appear at the start of several lines on the left.

> family about her, repeating her blessings and calling upon God to be merciful & help them when she was gone — She drew Father towards her and repeated to him a dream, *(many times)* which she had previously described as very consolatory & having followed her in all her better moods, so vivid as to be more like a vision she would say than a dream — it was produced by the impression made soon after the death of Aunt Ally's little children in '34, by an Allegory called the "Dark River" — three children were coming to meet him and bear him on to the "most beautiful glorious light which shone on the hills beyond" & it was a "beautiful light *(waving her hand in front of her face as she spoke)* & her whole expression of [warmth?] & continued as though it were there before her eyes) like the light of the moon but a thousand times brighter" — and just as I was to pass into the river I was obliged to come back to this dark troublesome world. — Then her expression became anxious as if looking for something, when asked, she replied "yes every now & then I remember something some verses come to my mind to comfort me — That — what is it?" "Vital Spark." and my aunt Ally continued the hymn, when she came to the lines "Hark, they whisper, angels say — Sister Spirit come away" "oh yes" that's so apropos
