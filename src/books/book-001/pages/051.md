A single sheet of lined paper closely written in faded brown ink, in cursive.

> She was much gratified by Cousin Esther's coming & afterwards Cousins Robt & Theodore Gourdin's. Speaking of their coming so far to see her & saying then as continually, during her sickness "God is so merciful, so merciful to me" — and again, "why is every body so anxious to see me, each one finds something new for my comfort" as though it were a strange thing that she should receive love & devotion at the hands of those to whom her life had been given." — Standing beside her I asked if she was quite comfortable "yes my daughter so happy, so happy" During an interval of ease when her natural cheerfulness returned. — Father said "why Esther there seems nothing for us to do for you now, unless it be to wish 10 years added to your life" "Oh no" was the reply "[bitter?] go over if I could live, I might not have all these blessings about me". Once after suffering a great deal of pain — she turned to those beside her "My children pray for me that my faith fail not" —
