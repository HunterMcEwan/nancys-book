> Hendersonville, N.C.
> November 22nd 1929.

> Dear "Ellie":
>
> How I appreciated that letter of yours. It came just before I left ~~[illegible struck-through passage]~~. I was not surprised for I had not counted on them strongly, and have always felt that nothing but a prolonged rest with outdoor work would solve the problem. ~~If we~~ I have been cutting wood, and dieting *(with caret-inserted text)*, my diet and feel much better already, and after 8 or 9 months I believe my eyes will be completely cured.
>
> ~~I saw~~ Uncle Pete the day before yesterday, and he seems as cheerful as ever. He and Frank came over to see us last Sunday, and we enjoyed their visit tremendously. James looks much better and seems in good spirits; we have a fine time together and ~~there~~ had no trouble at all with our meals. *[after he?]* Sunday[?].
>
