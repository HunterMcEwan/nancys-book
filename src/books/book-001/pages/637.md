notes: "Loose sheet of laid paper, brown ink in a tidy 19th-century hand at top with a second, looser hand in lighter ink at the bottom half. This is the third (final) page of Minnie P. FitzSimons's 1924 transcription from John L. Hadley's family Bible (continues from pp. 635–636). The upper section records births of enslaved persons in the household; the lower section is a separate explanatory note in a different hand, signed (presumably) by Minnie. 'Note 9' is pencilled at the lower left. The list of enslaved persons is recorded as the original Bible record; the page is preserved verbatim for the historical record."

**Births of negroes belonging to J. L. Hadley and family.**

(Faithful and beloved house servants of
Maugerie) M. P. F. 1924.

Selina daughter ~~Jimmy~~ and Daphne —
born 4th March 1841.

Elizabeth daughter of Nancy, born
27th April 1841.

January son of Selina born 11th March
1843.

Mary, son of Selina, born 28th February 1845.

John the child of Adamson and Pleasant,
born at Goose Creek on 14th Feb., 1842.

---

*[In a second, looser hand below:]*

My great grand father's second wife was a
widow — Maria Ann Mauger — (born ~~Maria~~ Mauger).
She had been a Hadley and there were more
people [than] my mother from the old Hadley Bibles.
After Mr. Edward Perry died his widow married
for the 3rd time Col. Thomas Fuller from Beaufort.

note 9.
