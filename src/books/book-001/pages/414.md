A pale envelope addressed in a strong slanted hand in pencil, with two French postage stamps (a beach scene at $1.00 and a $0.30 cityscape) and a circular postmark reading **58 NEVERS R.P. / 19.30 / 11 - 4 - 1972**. Below in blue ink: **Wedding Invitation of Dee Corbett and Claud Diamond** *(or similar — the names are not entirely clear; what is certain is "Wedding Invitation of …")* with a circled "1".

**Upper right — obituary of Amy FitzSimons Walker, clipped from *The Florida Times-Union*, Jacksonville, Monday, December 3, 1973:**

A masthead strip: **THE FLORIDA TIMES-UNION, JACKSONVILLE, MONDAY, DECEMBER 3, 1973**, and beside it the strip **24 — Jacksonville Journal, Monday, December 3, 1973**.

Below, headed **DEATH NOTICES**:

> WALKER — Funeral services for Mrs. Amy FitzSimons Walker, widow of James Pickens Walker, a former executive of the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad, will be held at the graveside in Evergreen Cemetery this afternoon. The Rev. Robert A. Bluner, rector of St. Mary's Old-the-Angels Episcopal Church of the Good Shepherd, will officiate. Funeral services will be held at the Frankfin Cole Funeral Home and at the graveside in Evergreen Cemetery this afternoon. Mrs. Walker was a native of Charleston, S.C., and the daughter of the late Christopher and Catherine FitzSimons. She made her home in Jacksonville for more than 40 years before moving to Orlando in 1972. Mrs. Walker was a communicant of the Church of the Good Shepherd and she was a member of the Daughters of the Colonial Wars. She was an honorary life member of the Florida Federation of Music Clubs. Survivors include a son, J. Pickens Walker, Jr., of Jacksonville; five grandchildren and three great-grandchildren. The family requests that flowers be omitted.

Pencilled beneath the clipping:

> Died Dec. 1, 1973

**Upper right — handwritten note (in modern blue pen, evidently a later editor's annotation):**

> Amy FitzSimons Walker, the compiler of this book, requested that she only have a small notice in the obituary column when she died.
>
> The notice was in error. It should have read that she was the widow of James Pickens Walker, a former executive of the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad, and mother of James Pickens Walker, Jr., a former news editor of the Florida Times-Union.

*[Annotation in a later hand, correcting the published obituary.]*

**Center — obituary of Mary Ann Walker McEwan, *Sentinel Star*, Orlando, Monday, February 10, 1975:**

A double-column clipping with header strip "**6-B   Sentinel Star   Monday, February 10, 1975**" and below a small bold-headed obituary:

> **McEWAN, MRS. MARY ANN WALKER** — Funeral services for Mrs. Mary Ann Walker McEwan of 1108 E. Cucara St. who passed away Saturday, will be held in the Cathedral Chapel Tuesday at 2:00 P.M., with Father Robert A. Bluner of St. Mary of the Angels Episcopal Church officiating. A native of Charleston, S.C., Mrs. McEwan came to Orlando in the mid-1940's from Jacksonville. She was the daughter of the late Mr. and Mrs. James Pickens Walker, and the wife of Oswald Beverly McEwan of Orlando. Central Dames of America. Survivors: husband, Oswald Beverly McEwan, of Orlando; two sons, Christopher G. McEwan of Columbus Falls, Mont., and James W. McEwan, of Orlando, two grandsons, Christopher G. Green and James G. Green; a daughter, Mrs. Nancy McEwan Green of Orlando; her grandsons. The service will be made by Mary's favorite charity. Arrangements are under the direction of the Franklin Cole Funeral Directors, Cary Hand Chapel (S. W. Pine St., Orlando, FL 32801).

> Mrs. Mary Ann W. McEwan

**Lower right — *The Barbour Democrat* clipping:**

A single column without dateline visible:

> **THE BARBOUR DEMOCRAT**
>
> Word was received this week from Mrs. J. Pickens Walker, Jr. of Jacksonville, Fla. concerning the death of her sister-in-law, Mary Ann Walker McEwan of Orlando, Fla. Mrs. McEwan was the niece of the late Mrs. Ann Modisette and the daughter of the late Mr. and Mrs. J. Pickens Walker, Sr.
>
> Last spring, Mrs. Ewan suffered a fall and was later paralyzed and did not recover from this. She died on February 7.

**Far lower right — *Florida Times-Union*, Jacksonville, Thursday, February 13, 1975:**

Header strip: **FLORIDA TIMES-UNION, JACKSONVILLE, THURSDAY, FEBRUARY 13, 1975**.

> **McEwan Funeral Held Here**
>
> Funeral services for Mrs. Mary Ann Walker McEwan, a former resident of Jacksonville, were held Tuesday in Evergreen Cemetery in Jacksonville with the Rev. Robert A. Bluner, rector of St. Mary's Old-the-Angels Episcopal Church in Orlando, officiating.
>
> Mrs. McEwan died Saturday in Orlando, **Feb. 7**.
>
> Mrs. McEwan was the sister of the late Pickens Walker, who was for many years the news editor of The Florida Times-Union.
>
> A native of Charleston, S.C., she was the daughter of the late Amy F. and James Pickens Walker. Mr. Walker was a director of Florida Publishing Co. and an employee of the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad.
>
> Mrs. McEwan lived in Jacksonville and Savannah prior to moving to Orlando in 1941.
>
> In Orlando, she was a member of the Junior League and the Rosalind Club. She was a communicant of St. Luke's Episcopal Cathedral in Orlando.
>
> Survivors include a daughter, Mrs. Nancy McEwan Green of Orlando; two sons, Christopher G. McEwan of Columbus Falls, Mont., and James W. McEwan of Orlando; a granddaughter; and two grandsons.
