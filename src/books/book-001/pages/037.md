A two-page spread. The left side holds the end of one handwritten letter (continued from the preceding scan); the right side begins a new letter dated April 18, 1880 from Mulberry. Both are in brown ink. Several pencilled annotations run along the upper margins.

## Top margin annotations (left page)

> *[Pencilled note across the top of the left page]:* Mr. [Williman] Onslow & his sister Catherine Barker, my grand aunts. Sis & Ellen [were?] my grand mothers 1st cousins [F.M.E.?]

## Left page (continuation of earlier letter)

> [...] no other children. Precious eyes & hair are lovely, & Annie is so like what her Mother was at her age as possible. I don't think I told you that Bertie & Salter Robertson are both to be married.
>
> Mrs. Motte still comes very frequently in her basket buggy & is bright, & very nice in lending us books. She let us see Robert's Drawings & they are most interesting—his talent is very unusual. He was delighted with them — Robert sent up to the Hall a card basket on the sides of which she has made pencil drawings & Tody was so much pleased at the skill they showed that he secured the basket, & hopes to send it to the Art Association in N.
