> to expect them never to cry. Christie is devoted to dancing
> and every evening after tea all of the negroes and the children
> collect in the play room and have the noisiest dancing and
> singing you ever heard. I offered to attend the Ball one
> night, but Christie said that would not do, as he knew I would
> be a restraint on "dam boy and gal" who form the orchestra.
> I believe the servants enjoy the children as much almost as
> we do. Sam and Theo patronize Thomas. Thee has learnt to
> say "Whe Uh Tom" when he comes in the hall and does not find
> Thomas, just as Sam does. Christie does not care to sit in
> laps but Thomas says when they are out walking with him his
