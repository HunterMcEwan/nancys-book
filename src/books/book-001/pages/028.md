## Upper center: photograph and clipping (Master of Medway)

A halftone newspaper portrait of an elderly white-bearded man in jacket and tie. Caption beneath, in italic capitals:

> **CAPT. SAMUEL G. STONEY**
>
> *S. G. S.*

Below the portrait, a newspaper clipping in two narrow columns:

> ### Master of Medway
>
> *R. I. P.*
>
> Master of Medway, the Flintlock is waking,
> Glinting with dew at the rising of dawn;
> The mist on the marsh is bright with daybreaking,
> The bayhorses wait, silver are shining and shaking,
> The hunters are coming, with hound and with horn.
>
> Master of Medway, the wild birds are singing,
> The tall pines are tracking their tapers of the sky,
> Far into Wampencha the mallards are winging,
> So down the old creek-road on hunters come swinging
> With laughter and jesting and hopes that are high.
>
> Master of Medway, ask old dogs is started
> Down to the Pasturous impatient to lay,
> Fearfully much of the wild ducks left, [illegible] hearted,
> They are loved Master from us too departed,
> And from life's farm has gone hither away.
>
> Master of Medway! You've gone as before me
> We to Life's River broadens shall roam.
> Hear how the live-oaks' chorus you bear me;
> Hark are sleep shall, gladness restore me—
> When the day's done, to rejoin you at Home.
>
> *— Archibald Rutledge.*
>
> *Mont Pleasant, Pa.,*
> *July 26, 1937.*
