A composite album page with a manuscript pedigree across the top, photographs down the left and lower center, a printed wedding announcement at center-right, and a newspaper clipping at the far right edge.

**Top — pedigree in ink, three generations:**

> **Mary Ann FitzSimons** b. Sept. 30th 1899 — m. Nov. 5th 1920 — Christ Church — Adams Run, S.C.
>
> **Donald McKay Allston** — Aug. 7th 1889
>
> 1) **Amy Perry — B. Aug. 22nd 1921 — m. May 16th 1946 — Henry Bunzon Fishbourne at Willtown Bluff Plantation**
>   1) Henry Bunzon Fishbourne 2nd — D. May 26th 1948
>   2) Donald Allston Fishbourne B. Aug. 13th 1951
>
> 2) **Anacaitlin Theodora — B. Nov. 218 1923 — m. Robert Hale McEwan — Dec. 8th 1945 — St. Phillip's Church Charleston, S.C.**
>   1) Robert Hale McEwan B. July 13th 1947 — Orlando Fla.
>   2) Amy Allston McEwan B. March 4, 1949.
>
> 3) **Donald McKay 2nd B. Feb. 21st 1925 — m. Margarita Consuelo FitzSimons — June 21st 1947 — Hendersonville N.C.**
>   1) Donald McKay Allston III B. Oct. 30th 1948
>   2) Margarita Consuelo Allston B. Aug. 19th 1951
>   3) Mary Ann Allston B. Jan 4th 1957 — D. Aug 21 1962
>   4) Daniel FitzSimons Allston B. July 5 — 1965

[Numbering and one or two dates in the original are slightly muddled — the original page shows occasional erasures and over-writings.]

**Photographs (clockwise from upper left):**

A small sepia portrait of a curly-haired toddler in a white frock, captioned beneath:

> Amy Allston

Below that, a small black-and-white photograph of a one-story house among trees (no caption).

Below that, a sepia bust portrait of a small child in striped jumper, captioned in pencil:

> [Anacaitlin] Allston

To the right of these, a larger sepia photograph of three young children — a girl seated at left, a small boy seated at center, a girl standing at right — in white dresses and short suit. No caption.

Below the group portrait, a small full-length photo of a man in suit and hat standing beside a fence; pencil caption partly illegible.

**Centre-right — printed wedding announcement** (engraved on cream card mounted at center):

> Howard and Harriett Fishbourne Roy Lawrence
> request the honour of your presence at the marriage of their daughter
> *Mary Ann*
> to
> the *Donald McKay Allston*
> on Wednesday, the third of November
> nineteen hundred and twenty
> at twelve o'clock noon
> Christ Church Parish
> Adams Run, South Carolina

[Some words in the engraved card are abbreviated or styled in italic in the original; the central names are set larger.]

**Lower center — head-and-shoulders portrait of a young girl with plaits:**

> Mary Ann Allston, 1961

**Far right — narrow newspaper clipping (bordered):**

> **Ancestral Home Given Mrs. Allston By Whitney Widow**

[Body of clipping is a several-paragraph article in newsprint; at this scan resolution the body text is too small to reliably transcribe. Visible phrases include references to a Charleston historic property and to the Whitney family. The article appears to date from the late 1970s or 1980s.]
