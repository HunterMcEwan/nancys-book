**Headline:**

> **Episcopal**

> **Ordained**

> By PETER R. GENTILE

> Staff Reporter

> The Rev. Dr. C. FitzSimons Allison Sunday became the Episcopal Church's newest bishop in a colorful ceremony at Gaillard Municipal Auditorium.
>
> About 1,500 persons watched as he was formally installed as bishop coadjutor of the Diocese of South Carolina. He will become bishop of the diocese upon the retirement of the current bishop, the Rt. Rev. Gray Temple.
>
> About 200 prelates, priests, and others participated in the colorful ceremony, which was broadcast over the city's cable television system.
>
> After Dr. Allison was formally presented, his brother, Dr. James Allison Jr., read the Epistle.
>
> Dr. Allison's 17-year-old son, Sidney, read the New Testament lesson, and his sister, Mrs. Rodgers Jr., dean of Trinity Episcopal School for Ministry, delivered the sermon, in which he urged Dr. Allison to "do …"

> A native of Columbia, Dr. Allison received his early education in the Columbia public schools. He attended The Citadel for a year before going to Sewanee, where he earned a degree from Virginia Theological Seminary and his Ph.D. from Oxford University in Great Britain. He received an honorary doctor of divinity degree from the University of the South in 1978.
>
> He served in July in the U.S.
