A genealogical and photographic page. Two oval-framed studio portraits at the top — a bearded man at left and a young woman at right — flank a central block of handwritten notes. Four further portraits sit in a row across the lower half of the page, each captioned beneath in blue ink.

**Top heading (centered, between the two upper portraits):**

> Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons — B. Dec. 28th 1856 — D. Oct 18th 1930
> M. April 13th 1887
> Mary Ann Perry — B. July 14th 1859 — D. Jan. 8th 1934

**Above the left portrait, in pencil:**

> Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons

**Above the right portrait, in pencil:**

> Mary Ann Perry

**Children list (centered, in blue ink):**

> 1) Amy Perry — B. Feb. 4th 1888
> 2) Theodore Barker — B. July 8th 1890 — D. March 26th
> 3) Duncan Wragg Sr. — B. May 8th 1892 — D. Feb. 4th 1895
> 4) Thomas Parker — B. Nov. 8th 1894 — D. May 17th 1896
> 5) Samuel Cantell — B. Aug. 20th 1897 — D. July 21st 1898
> 6) Mary Ann — B. Sept. 30th 1899
> 7) Samuel Gaillard — B. Sept. 21st 1904 — D. Oct. 20th 1961

**Editorial paragraph (handwritten, beneath the children list):**

> The following is the inscription on the tombstone of Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons in Magnolia Cemetery — Chas. S.C.
>
> "He scarcely used to doff his pride or slough the dross of earth, the path he trod today to God, he walked it from the birth, In simpleness, gentleness, honor and clean mirth."
>
> His wife and son Theodore Barker FitzSimons are buried in the same lot in Magnolia Cemetery.
