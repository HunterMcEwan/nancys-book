A genealogical and photographic page. Two oval-framed studio portraits at the top — a bearded man at left and a young woman at right — flank a central block of handwritten notes. Four further portraits sit in a row across the lower half of the page, each captioned beneath in blue ink.

**Top heading (centered, between the two upper portraits):**

> Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons — B. Dec 23rd 1855 — D. Oct 18th 1931
> M. April 19th 1879
> Mary Ann Perry — B. July 19th 1859 — D. Jan 9th 1934

**Above the left portrait, in pencil:**

> Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons

**Above the right portrait, in pencil:**

> Mary Ann Perry

**Children list (centered, in blue ink):**

> Amy Perry — B. Feb. 14th 1880
> 2) Theodore Barker — B. July 27th 1860 — D. March 26th
> 3) Isadora May Dn. — B. Aug 17th 1872 — D. July 4th 1875
> 4) Spencer Pickens — B. Sept 27th 1874 — D. May 19th 1876
> 5) Samuel Cantell — B. Aug 20th 1882 — D. July 14th 1888
> 6) Mary Ann — B. Sept 30th 1878
> 7) Samuel Gaillard — B. Sept 21st 1902 — D. 22-10-1961

**Editorial paragraph (handwritten, beneath the children list):**

> The following is the inscription on the tombstone of Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons in Magnolia Cemetery:—
>
> "He served until [illegible] his pride is doing the duties of earth — He gave to God all glory & God, he received of all the lustre — In simple [illegible] gentleness, love, and clean breath." His wife and son Theodore Barker FitzSimons are buried in the same lot in Magnolia Cemetery.
