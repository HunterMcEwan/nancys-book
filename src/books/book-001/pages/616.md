notes: "Typescript copy, fifth page of the will of Ann Drayton Perry. Page numbered '#5' at upper right; '792' written in pencil at corner. Page sets out trust provisions for the Charleston house and lot in the event of the consummated marriage of Edward D. Perry and Maria Ann Mauger, and the subsequent passage to their issue or grandchildren in case the marriage fails, ifsue dies before either parent, or the intended wife dies or remarries. Begins the bequest of two plantations at Horse Savannah in the Parish of Saint Paul (the Blue House and Old House) with the enslaved people attached, including Jony, Mary, Anthony Isaac and his wife. Transcribed literally for archival fidelity."

But should either of my grand children depart this life before the death of my said son or termination of the widowhood of his said intended wife as the case may be leaving ifsue such ifsue shall in such event represent his her or their deceased parent and take among them the share or proportion which such Parent would have been entitled to if living Provided neverthelefs that if the aforesaid intended marriage between my son and the said **Maria Ann Mauger**, shall not take effect, or if in that case upon the death of my said mother, the aforesaid House and Lot, shall go to my son during his life, and from and after his death to my said grand children and the ifsue of any deceased grand child as above particularly limitted, and specified and to their heirs and afsigns forever.
