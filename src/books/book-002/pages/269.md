She notes that the baby wore a christening dress that had belonged to 'Pick' (the recipient, J. Pickens Walker Jr.) and that her mother ('Pray' — the writer's pet name for Mary Ann Walker McEwan, the recipient's sister) used the dish that had been used at 'Christ. Pick' (Pick's own christening). The continuity of family christening relics across three generations — from the recipient's own infancy ca. 1912 (see pages 070–072) to the great-grandchild — is the substance of this page."

The second leaf of the letter, continuing in blue ink.

> end. He wore a dress that had belonged to Puck and Dear Pray used the dish that was used at Christ. Pick. That certainly made it mean so much more.
>
> He is growing like a weed. He weighs about 8½ lbs. and is 20½ inches long. That is quite a sizable gain from the 5½ lbs. he weighed at birth and was only 19" long. He has just started smiling and laughing. It is such

*[The names 'Puck' and 'Pray' are family pet-names: 'Puck' (or possibly 'Pick') here apparently refers to the recipient James Pickens Walker Jr. as a baby — the christening dress that 'had belonged to' him — and 'Dear Pray' is the writer's mother, Mary Ann Walker McEwan, sister of the recipient. 'Christ. Pick' = the christening of Pick (J. P. Walker Jr.), ca. 1912.]*
