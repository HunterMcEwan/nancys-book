notes: "First page of a child's letter, written in pencil cursive on plain paper, dated 'Spartanburg / Oct. 18th 1900' and signed (on page 023) 'Amy P. FitzSimons'. The writer — Amy Perry FitzSimons, then about eight years old — agrees to 'stand for' her correspondent Helen (i.e. be a bridesmaid or attendant). She mentions a recent visit by 'Miss Emmie' who passed through Spartanburg without their knowing it, and reports family news: the baby trying to walk and talk, a new black kitten, and an afternoon helping the people pick cotton."

> Spartanburg
> Oct. 18th 1900
>
> Dear Helen
>
> I received your letter yesterday and I will be very glad to stand for you. I have grown a little this summer, I think that it is about time, do you not think so too? Miss Emmie passed through here the other day, we did not know that she was to pass through and so we did not see her. I suppose that Harriott is to stand with me.
