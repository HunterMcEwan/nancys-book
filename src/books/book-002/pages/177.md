*[Page 2 of the letter, marked '·2·' at the top:]*

> Mrs. Walker you was such a lovely Madam to work for. positive but Nice of corse. I knew my place as a Servent. I just like ya all so much I dont want to work for you smile. What I mean is I just want to please you so well I just cant think I could fail. you know you nat tall the same person. I know my place but you know I am older and nurses and the last two sets of peeple that I work for was peeple that they sawney like you would thank they Never had no servent yet rich as cream they cep me upset and fussing back at them and Talking back I beleive is habit with nu now, thay just Ruined me but I beleive I [Caused] my manners Look with the [last?] peoples. I wanted a job in Savanah all I had to say I worked for mr. J. P. Walker I need not tell you what the name walker ment in Savanah. I would call your name so often some Time peopel would say

*[Page 3, marked '3':]*

> I dont see why you dident fallow the Walkers you wont got noBig with Noone Else. you all was fair and true and frank with good understanding. you were the most understanding lady I ever worked for. that is why I am satisfied that you know that if I ever get a help of charce I am coming and work for ya all. Edith I can like ya all very much she remembers how nice ya all were to let me raise her in the Corner in The Kitchen and when she had The medson you tald the dorey to let us know wile and see if the nice things ya all did. I am stronger mrs walker to say but I havent ben in Town since may the first. I went to the dinest and he scared me for two mantes and since that I have ben dieng and it is hard to get any there and when you do they are in a hurry to come Lasee, no, they havent get a Single park for colered to res in. but I hope to come to see ya all soon I am sure you are tired of reading this letter.
