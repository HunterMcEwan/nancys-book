Oak hurst was my garden — The house had been burned during the Civil war — The story goes that when Bld that the Northern soldiers came at *[once]* to Oak hurst Mr. Barnwell refused to come out side — The officer in charge told him that they were going to burn the house — but still the old gentleman remained seated in the hall. No one would persuade him to leave his seat — so finally the officer ordered his men to go in and bring him out chair and all — But the remains of the old garden were still there. Japonica trees — big enough to climb to peek at the blooms — spurea — sweet shrub — & one azalea bush — and fields that bloomed in the most unexpected places. They were all that was left of the pre-war "Oak hurst". They had no care — & the trees were a tangle of vines and undergrowth.

It was only a short walk from Mt. Hope to Oakhurst & in the Spring I went there when ever I could.
