Beneath the photo cluster is a multi-line pencil narrative by the compiler about Ille and Bob, the child's parents — Bob's medical training in Richmond, his internship in Norfolk (Conn.), and his army service from June 1941 (Ft. Belvoir, Ft. Beauregard, the 82nd Airborne). The photographs all show the same little boy — Robert L. Carlisle III — at various stages from infancy through about 18 months, sometimes alone, sometimes with an older man. Several caption initials read 'R.L.C. III' (and possibly 'R.S.C. III' in one); the child's name has been standardized as Robert L. Carlisle III in metadata."

**Photo captions (clockwise from upper left):**

Upper left — an infant in a long white gown sitting up, holding a stuffed lamb. Captioned beneath:

> Robert Lawrence
> Carlisle III — 1941

Upper middle — a small child standing beside a basket of stuffed animals. Captioned:

> R. L. Carlisle III 1st
> Xmas 1941

Upper right — a stooping older man (in coat and tie) bending toward a small child in overalls who holds a stick or toy gun in a garden setting. Captioned beneath:

> R. L. C. III Norfolk Conn.
> 1942

Lower left — a small child in a knitted suit walking beside an older man (also in coat and tie) in a garden; a wheeled toy or stroller is in the foreground. Captioned:

> R. S. C. III + J. P. W. 1942 Portsmouth Va

Lower middle — a small child in a smocked dress standing on a lawn. Captioned:

> R. S. C. III 18 months

**Narrative beneath the photographs (in pencil):**

Ille & Bob lived in Richmond until Bob finished medical school. He then interned in Norfolk Conn. He finished his internship in June 1941 — and went into service. He was stationed at Ft. Belvoir + then at Ft. Beauregard. He joined the 82nd air borne.

*[Some caption initials read "R. L. C." (Robert Lawrence Carlisle III) and some read "R. S. C." — the discrepancy is likely the compiler's pen variation rather than a different child, since the photographs follow one child in sequence. "J. P. W." in the Portsmouth caption is presumably James Pickens Walker (Sr. or Jr.). "Ille" is the compiler's spelling of the child's mother's nickname.]*
