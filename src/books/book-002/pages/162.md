**Newspaper clipping (upper left):**

> **Marriage of Miss Walker**
> **To Mr. Corbell Announced**
>
> Mr. and Mrs. J. Pickens Walker announce the marriage of their daughter, Emma Dee, to Robert Lawrence Corbell Jr., of Portsmouth, Va., son of Dr. and Mrs. Robert Lawrence Corbell of Portsmouth, on October 8, 1937 in Amelia, Va. The couple will reside in Richmond, Va.
>
> Mrs. Corbell, formerly of Savannah, Ga., attended Hollins College. Mr. Corbell was graduated from Virginia Polytechnic Institute.

**Envelope (center):**

A pale tan, slightly creased envelope, postmarked at Richmond, Va. (circular cancellation reading **RICHMOND / AUG 9 / 11 AM / 1940 / VA.**) and bearing a 3-cent purple Jefferson Presidential-series stamp at the upper right.

Addressed in green-blue fountain-pen ink in a sloping cursive:

> Mrs. J. Peckens Walker
> c/o Mrs. Anna W. Modisette
> Philippi,
>     West Virginia.

*[The sender — "Dee" — is Emma Dee Walker Corbell, the compiler's daughter, writing from Richmond to her mother who was summering with Anna W. Modisette in Philippi, W.Va. The letter inside is preserved across the following pages (163–168). The envelope writer has spelled the family name "Peckens" rather than "Pickens," a slip of the pen.]*
