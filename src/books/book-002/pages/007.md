was called "membranous croup" in those days — diphtheria really — and in those days he would have lived — In those days he choked to death — The night that he was dying, in paroxysms of strangling — Dad was holding him — He held him close and Mammie heard him say — "Father son I will go with you just as far as God will let me!"

Hunkin died during the early morning of Feb 4th — my birthday — Bull and I did not know that he had died — of course we had no realization of death — Soon after we walked — the door between the rooms opened and Mammie was at Toilet there — my birthday present in her hands — I can see her as plainly as though she were still standing there — A pale gray flannel wrapper feather stitched in blue wool — dark hair piled high on her head — Heart broken — but not allowing her grief to conquer her. Calm and quiet — *[so]* her grief was hers and she was not going to let it frighten the children. I must have realized the wonder fulness of her even then — for it all so clearly remembered.

The nursery was our being doom — but ruled by the firm hand of a Mammie — Becky was the first — and Mammie Hannah came next. We loved them — and the thing that I realize in looking back —
