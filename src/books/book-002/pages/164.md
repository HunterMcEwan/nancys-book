notes: "Second page of the letter begun on page 163. The writer reports throwing away the china price list and needing to make another trip to Miller & R's (almost certainly Miller & Rhoads, the Richmond department store) before settling on a pattern, and remarks on Richmond residents' excitement about the floods — likely the August 1940 floods that affected much of Virginia and the upper South after heavy rains. The office where she works is only a couple of blocks from the rising water but apparently still accessible on foot. Written in graphite pencil on the same cream notepaper as the preceding leaf; the page breaks mid-sentence at 'and as'."
