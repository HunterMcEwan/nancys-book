Trouser legs, and a nurse's big white apron! I think that must be my first memory — I have a vague picture of a board wall — a white building — men's voices — my father's daughter — and the long skirts of a baby dress. It must have been I Theodore (Bula) Christine — *[illegible]* was christened in the church in Sandersville N.C. My grand mother Perry had a home there.

Mammie Becky was the nurse. She had nursed me & was with me for a long time. Becky was a wonderful person — a splendid nurse — full of fun, but strict. I always had to mind my manners and be a "little lady". I have never ceased to love her.

Becky was in Charleston when Pickens was born and came to see me. I asked her how old she was & she said — "having I knit nightly know — Bull I does know this — I was 3 days old the last time Genl. Lafayette came to Chas'."
