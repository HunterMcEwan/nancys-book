I don't know just when we began dating.

I knows I was going to Columbia for the commencement at U.S.C. — I had dates with Douglas McKay. Puck — Harrell and Henry Lawrence went up on the train with me — [too]. When we got to Columbia I went to stay at Aunt Kate's. My Columbia friends took charge of Puck — and on the [unforgettable] occasion when he climbed a tree in Capital Square after a squirrel — I don't remember if he was able to get to the dance next night or not. But the boys were delighted over what they had done.

We became engaged in June. I had unsalaried Junior That [summer] now I.S.O. was sent up to Brookland. Puck used to come for week ends when ever he could. This picture was taken in the woods that summer.

The following winter Puck was sent to tea — under Mr. McHardy. He stayed with the McHardy's. We were to be married in the fall of 1908. The
