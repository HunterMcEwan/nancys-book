Narrative beneath:

Samuel Cadwalader Walker was born in Winchester Va. 1842. Later the family moved to Fairmont W. Va. then to Clarksburg W. Va. At 18 he joined the Union army — "to preserve the Union." He married Louisa Ida Pickens in 1877. They lived in Clarksburg and he travelled for [Pennsylvanian] of Baltimore. Anna was born in Clarksburg May 1878. They moved to the farm Fairfield — which [Park] here given to her by the father James Pickens — in 1879.

James Pickens Walker was born at Fairfield April 27th 1883.

In 1889 Mr. Walker went to Charleston W. Va. and opened a large book store. Later he came back to the farm. The book store venture not being successful. In 1893 he entered Gov. service as immigrant inspector at Seattle Wash. In that same capacity he moved to Los Angeles & Tacoma, & Havre Mont. where he died in 1923. and is buried in Philippi W. Va.

Mrs. S. C. Walker continued to live on the farm. In 1895 she took the 2 children and went to Washington D.C. for the school year. She did this for 5 winters. Then Anna entered Gunston and Pickens went to Rock Hill Academy [in Wash-]
