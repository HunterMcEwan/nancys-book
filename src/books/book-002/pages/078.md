In blue ink at the top, continuing from page 77:

> That summer 1918 — I was pregnant. I [had?] an acute bronchitis and when I was well enough Dr. Gourrey made me go to the mountains. So Pickens and I went to Saluda and boarded with the Hutsons. They were wonderful people and became very dear friends. [Pickens] also had acute bronchitis and was desperately ill. They took her on a boat from Cain Island to a hospital in Sav. When her condition became so desperate the Hutsons made me leave Pickens with them and go to [meet?] him in Sav.

*[The pronouns slip between "he" and "her" — the writer treats Pickens (her son) as the subject throughout. The "boat from Cain Island" reference suggests a separate incident or that Amy is conflating two episodes.]*

Below the text at left, a small sepia snapshot of a small child in white standing under a parasol in tall grass. Caption beside it in blue ink:

> Pickens — Saluda — 1918.

To the right of the photograph, in blue ink:

> That fall Puck was sent to Charleston S. C. as superintendent of the Char. division of A. C. L.
>
> We moved there and we stayed at 132 Rutledge Ave. Ilee was born in Roper Hospital Oct 18th, 1918.

Two further snapshots at the bottom:

- Left: a Black woman in a white apron holding a fair-haired baby in a long white gown.
- Right: a Black woman in a long white apron standing in front of a house, holding a baby in white.

Caption between them, in blue ink:

> Ilee and
> Mary.
