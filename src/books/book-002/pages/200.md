notes: 'A single sheet of personal stationery printed at the top with "MRS. LOUIE D. SIMONDS / 48 Meeting St. / Charleston, S. C." The letter is written in a flowing but somewhat shaky cursive hand and signed "Mamie Simonds." It invites Amy to a golden wedding anniversary gathering on Monday December 11th, with the children hosting a family party, and notes that Helen Simonds and "the Hoover girls" will also attend. Harry Parker has offered to host Amy overnight. The letter is unsigned-dated but the reference to Dec. 11th as their golden wedding, together with the Portsmouth letters on adjacent pages, suggests 1944.'

> MRS. LOUIE D. SIMONDS
> 48 Meeting St.
> Charleston, S. C.

> Dear Amy,
>
> Would it be possible for you to be with us on Monday night — Dec. 11th — our golden wedding day. The children are having a family gathering with a few other friends and as many of our bridesmaids & groomsmen as we can get together. You & Helen Simonds were the flower girls. I met Harry Parker today & she said to tell you to spend the night with her. Do try to come. Let her know & let me know what train & some one will meet you.
>
> Love
> Mamie Simonds
