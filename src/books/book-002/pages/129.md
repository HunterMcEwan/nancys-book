A letter on **Southern Railway Company** preprinted letterhead (Form 812, "1-13-33 600M D.B.") with a blank line for "OFFICE OF". Dated upper right in pencil:

> 11/25/33

> Dear Folks:
>
> Just a note to make Mom mad!
>
> Went to a W. & L. alumni dinner in Lexington — it was talking to William J. Thru — his name was — Dean of Students. Dr. Keim said first words were a talk and that outlined "The Ideals of W. & L." — to develop integrity. — honor — self-reliance — personality — character.
>
> In my conversation with Dr. William I mentioned your son (as I generally do whenever I get the opportunity). He said:
>
> "You have great head Dr. Keim on the Ideals of W. & L. I am sure that any group of men who knows last year's student body would never Pickens Walker among the ten men who [feels?] the ideals of W. & L. as described by Dr. Keim."
>
> My best to the whole family
>
> [signed] *Pop.* *[or "Pickens"]*
>
> They told us last night that we are due a [licking?] this afternoon — Team shot — two men fined for hooking [hicking?] and three injured. — That was the trouble last Saturday —

*[Letter unsigned at this scan; the closing initial is partly torn. Pickens Walker Jr. was a student at Washington and Lee University in Lexington, Virginia, from 1929 to 1933. The Dr. Keim mentioned is almost certainly William J. Keim, then dean of students at W. & L.]*
