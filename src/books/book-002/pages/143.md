*[Continued from page 142.]*

…an *anguish stricken* voice — *"gradon are out!"* — another, with an *anguish shriek*: *"My God — I packed everything!"* screams *"Oh — oh Lordy!"* under I saw out it, then B's & C's I nearly had hysterics. The ruby nearly killed me! But — I sure[ly] was glad. Maybe I can have my soph standing next year anyway. Wasn't that an awful slight!

And Mummie guess what! It's *been snowing* for two days. Marlie and I have been out playing in it! You don't know what a thrill my first snow ball and snow man gave me! More fun! After [seeing] in the snow though, I decided to go swimming — so — there Jeff went in. *Miss Cheever* was a long *to me* and — she's *teaching* me all about it. We've decided to go in most every day; so we'll know something about it.

The Mr. Dramatic Club has started [rehearsals] on Saturday night from 7 to 8 on make-up, lighting, costuming, staging and directing. I really believe they're going to be good. Marlie and I are sending a missing *cheers* on Marlie as possible. *Great want!* Your little daughter is going to be a *"champion"* athlete and actress and student of this dug up anyway! She is a *"champion"* in *loving her mother* and — in *have the best mother in the world — Happy birthday, dearest mummie — I love you —*

Dee —

*[Sideways writing along the left margin, partly illegible — appears to continue with personal news and signatures.]*
