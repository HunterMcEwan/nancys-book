**Snapshot (upper left)** — A small color or hand-tinted photograph of a woman and a small boy standing on a flight of plain wooden exterior steps. The woman wears a dark winter coat and holds what appears to be a wrapped parcel; the boy, standing in front of her, wears a pale knitted cap and matching coat.

**Annotation, upper right column**, in blue ink:

> Clara Brinson Williams —
> "Brissy" — She was our maid in
> Jax. from 19[__] to 1941. A
> good friend as well as an
> alright maid. She married
> in 1941 and moved to Mrs. York [uncertain].
> I always hear from her at Xmas time. **A. P.** [?]

**Envelope (lower left)** — A small white envelope franked with a 3-cent purple stamp and circular postmark reading **Fort George, Fla.** (date partly illegible). The return address, hand-written in the upper-left corner in blue pencil:

> B. Saunders
> Fort George, Fla.

The face is addressed in blue ink, with several lines lined through and re-directed:

> Mrs. J. Pickens Walker
> ~~11[__] S. Main St.~~ *(struck through)*
> 3657 Peachmont St.
> Phillips P.O.
> Jacksonville, Florida

**Annotation, lower right column**, in blue ink:

> Letter from Edna
> who was with us
> for a long time
> in Sav. Ga.
> She writes to me
> every now & then. This
> letter was in ans. to
> one asking her if
> she would come to
> help with the
> grand children.

*[The letter from Edna Saunders, postmarked Fort George, Fla., is transcribed on page 176. "Sav. Ga." is Savannah, Georgia. The "grand children" — Mary Ann and Pickens Jr. — appear on the preceding pages.]*
