He had 3 daughters — Carrie — Judith and Annie & they came over to school with us. Their cousins George and Lichut Sasson came too. They were living at Prospect Hill.

Dad was a wonderful provider. There were two colored women — Stuel — Warren and gues word men — who did nothing but hunt and fish for "the house". We are wise sat an abundant table at the very best — Breakfasts fully served — and we underestimate Eulen — It was not unusual to have a turkey at one end of the table — a ham at the other — and a platter of wild ducks on the side — And what a meal break fast was — hominy — always — a platter of sausage — hog's head cheese — scrambled eggs — biscuits + waffles — or muffins and game salmi — always two hot breads — Uncle tells a story of being there once + Dad asked Buck to have some sausages. Buck's answer was "no thanks — I don't believe they agree with me very well — so I'd better stop with the 8 I have had".
