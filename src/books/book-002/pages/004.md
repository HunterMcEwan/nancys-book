first — and Mammie thought it was her bounden duty to make me perfect. She never became resigned to my being what I was, until I passed the whipping age.

Dad decided to plant for him self and bought Shoengaussen plantation — He and Mammie had made many plans for living there and leading their own lives — Then out of the blue — Uncle Kit bought Mt. Hope, Oak hurst and Gibbes. Dad was to plant there + Uncle Kit [to] finance them — until they paid for them selves. The brothers were devoted & loyal — Dad gave up moving to Shoengaussen — and went into partnership with Uncle Kit. "Mount Hope" — now known as "Willtown" — had been bought from the Grimballs. "Oak hurst" was the old Barnwell place — and "Rosemont" had belonged to the Gibbes —

Of the move from Rock Spring to Mt. Hope I have only the vaguest memory — confusion — Mammie in a big apron bearing some baskets — and of Bub running around with a cow bell tied around his neck — Mammie was so afraid of his getting into the river that she put the bell on him to keep track of his comings and goings.
