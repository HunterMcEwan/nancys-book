notes: "A large sepia-toned photographic print mounted to fill the album page, oriented sideways (the photo's top is at the left edge of the page). The image shows a bride at center in a long white gown with cathedral-length veil, flanked by approximately ten attendants in matching long pale gowns, each carrying a bouquet. The group is posed in front of an arched garden trellis or arbor. The print is a studio proof: written into the lower right corner of the image in white grease pencil are the words 'Proof / Property of / Dishinger-Woodward Studios.' This proof belongs with the Mary Ann Walker / Oswald Beverley McEwan wedding (14 May 1941) documented on pages 187–188. Phase 2 enhanced pass: studio name corrected from phase-1 'Dahmer-Woodward' to 'Dishinger-Woodward' (the same Woodward credited as photographer in the Jacksonville debutante clipping on page 145, 'Photographs of Misses Anthony and Peyton by Woodward')."
