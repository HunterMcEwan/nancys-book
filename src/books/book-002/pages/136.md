Three Savannah- and Jacksonville-paper clippings on the same page.

**Upper left clipping** — headed:

> **J. P. WALKER IS GIVEN PROMOTION**
>
> Becomes General Superintendent at Jacksonville
>
> **O. H. PAGE COMING HERE**
>
> Changes In Coast Line Effective Tomorrow

> Circulars issued by J. N. Brand, general manager of the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad Company, were received here yesterday, announcing effective tomorrow, the promotion of J. Pickens Walker to be general superintendent of the eastern division of the Coast Line, with headquarters in Jacksonville, to succeed R. A. McCranie, deceased.
>
> The circular also announced the appointment of O. H. Page to be general superintendent of the southern division, with headquarters at Savannah, to succeed Mr. Walker.

A head-and-shoulders portrait of Walker is inset to the right of the article, captioned beneath:

> Named to Succeed Late R. A. McCranie

**Lower-left clipping** — headed:

> **CAPT. J. P. WALKER**

> A popular railroad man has just left Savannah and while here he made many friends both in the line of his work and outside among the people generally.
>
> Capt. J. P. Walker has been transferred to Jacksonville after having been superintendent in Savannah of this section of the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad.
>
> He change of residence was because of his promotion, still we hate to lose a good citizen and city worker like Captain Walker. He was a member of the entire state in Savannah and worked for the good of his railway system and for the progress of Savannah.
>
> We can not be indifferent to removal of such a man from the city even though his removal brings to him added preferment in his railway work.
>
> While receiving the congratulations of his friends they recognize the loss to the city.

**Right-hand clipping (mounted sideways)** — headed:

> **FAREWELL DINNER TO J. P. WALKER**
>
> BY RAILROAD EMPLOYES
>
> Handsome Silver Present Given Him Last Night

> Men who have served under J. P. Walker in Savannah on the eastern coast line of the Atlantic Coast Line as a member of the Atlantic Coast Line and the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad as a friend, at a farewell dinner tendered him by Atlantic Coast Line Railroad employes at the hotel De Soto last evening.
>
> Mr. Walker has recently received a commission of the railroad with headquarters in Jacksonville, Fla. as assistant to J. R. Brand, vice president and general manager of the system. In addition to a presentation of testimonials, Mr. Walker received a handsome silver loving cup, which was presented to him as a token of esteem in which he is held by the men with whom he has been associated.
>
> J. Coyle Mixon presided at the testimonial dinner given in the cocktail room, in the absence of P. H. Tonalsey [Tonsley?], who was unable to attend. He extended the gratitude of the men assembled to Mr. Walker. Brief addresses were made by the [following?]: Geo. R. Beach, [Capt.?] L. T. Tate, Coast Line, company attorney; Mr. T. M. Sale, general freight agent, [and?] Mr. R. C. Norton, division superintendent of transportation. J. B. F. Hammond, then the Coast Line freight traffic department. T. T. McEachern, freight agent, Charleston, J. M. Bird, executive department. *[Capt.?]* R. P. Hamilton, recipient of the gift in the Coast Line, Charleston District; and others.
>
> On behalf of the engineers of the Coast Line, James L. Berry, [chief of?] engineers in Atlanta, was a speaker, paying tribute to Mr. Walker's farewell after eight years, where he attended as one of the railroad public utility men of the city.

**Side clippings (reverse text showing through, partly visible):**

> SAUSSY IN ATLANTA ON FARM PROJECT

> DISABLED VETERANS WILL RUN DRIVE
> Forget-Me-Nots to Be Sold On November 3rd

> CRIMINAL INTERESTS FEAR ALL REHABILITATION PLAN

*[These reverse-side headlines are present on the verso of the right-hand clipping and on the cut at lower right; they are not the principal subject of the page.]*

*[James Pickens Walker, father of Pickens Walker Jr., was an Atlantic Coast Line Railroad executive whose career took him from Savannah to Jacksonville. The clippings document his promotion to general superintendent of the eastern division at Jacksonville, succeeding R. A. McCranie. The farewell dinner clipping is from the Savannah press and records the testimonial held in his honor at the De Soto hotel before his departure.]*
