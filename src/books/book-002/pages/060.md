notes: "Album page with a printed engraved wedding invitation card pasted at center. Handwritten memoir text in blue ink above and below the card continues Amy FitzSimons's narrative about the 1908 financial panic and her decision to marry despite it. The card is an engraved 'At Home' invitation from Mr. and Mrs. Samuel Gaillard FitzSimons for the marriage of their daughter Amy Perry to James Pickens Walker at Adams Run, S.C."

Above the card, in blue ink:

> The Spring of 1908 the bottom fell out — no work — every thing at a standstill. Puck was laid off — with the understanding that the first work that came up would be given him. We went ahead and were married Oct 20th 1908.

The engraved invitation, in formal script:

> Mr. and Mrs. Samuel Gaillard Fitz Simons
> request the honor of
> your presence at the marriage of their daughter
>
> Amy Perry
> to
> Mr. James Pickens Walker
> on the evening of the twentieth of October
> nineteen hundred and eight
> at nine o'clock
> At Home
> Adams Run, South Carolina

Below the card:

> Harrell Gurney was our best man and Harry Sass my maid of honor.
