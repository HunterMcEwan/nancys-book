A pencil-written letter on lined notebook paper, filling the page. Transcribed preserving the writer's spelling and punctuation:

> Fort George, Fla
> Sept. 12, 1944
>
> My dear Mrs Walker — how are
> you and Family well I hope.
> I am well but ailing. I haven't
> felt well for some time altho I
> am not sick. just taking lots a [uncertain]
> all of the time. lazy. lies and [uncertain] cofel [uncertain]
> about what I eat on account of gas
> all of the time. I think of you all
> all of the time just as if I was one of you.
> Please write and tell me how Miss
> Dee on Miss Mandy [uncertain] is. how is lovely [uncertain]
> Little Robert is and chubby little
> Chris's. they are so sweet. I can't
> express just what I think about them.
> I can see Chris's little old chubby
> hand eating supper or dinner that
> day. and little Robert told me
> his name so sweet. he is charming
> and sweet like his mother. she is
> just as pretty as ever. I don't think
> they would I'm afraid not having
> me around because I would forget and take
> them for the same sweet little
> girls just to know. smile!

*[The letter continues on subsequent pages; this is page one only. Edna Saunders writes from Fort George, Florida, replying to Mrs. Walker's invitation (described on page 175) that she come to Jacksonville to help with the grandchildren. "Little Robert" is presumably Robert Lawrence Cantwell III, photographed on page 174 as an infant in January 1940 and thus about four years old at the time of the letter; "Chris" is another child of the family whose surname is not given. Several words — chiefly mid-line nouns — are eccentrically spelled and read with low confidence; the writer's own punctuation and capitalization have been preserved.]*
