A **Western Union** telegram mounted sideways on the page, on a lined notebook sheet (three punch-holes at right). A small printed sticker at upper left reads:

> NATIONAL MOTHERS DAY WEEK
> [further illegible — "Don't forget to wire / your Congratulations / WESTERN UNION"]

The body of the telegram, in capitalized teletype lettering:

> CFA201 40 NL COLLECT=LEXINGTON VIR 1
> *[date stamp partly visible]*
>
> MRS J P WALKER=
> 112 EAST 52 SAVANNAH GA=
>
> PASSED ALL MY WORK WELL ENOUGH TO GET NINE EXTRA CREDITS WHUPEE FANCY DRESS WONDERFUL RECEIVED CHECK MADE THE SWIMMING TEAM AND GOT FIRST PLACE IN THE DIVING AGAINST STAUNTON MILITARY ACADEMY TODAY HG DOINGS HERE NOW WHUPEE AGAIN=
>
> PICKENS.

A small pencilled note at the lower edge of the album page, partly obscured by the telegram:

> [illegible — possibly "86" and "82 + 3"]

*[Pickens (James Pickens Walker Jr.) was a student at Washington and Lee University, Lexington, Virginia, during the period this telegram was sent. His mother, Amy FitzSimons Walker, was living at 112 East 52nd Street, Savannah, Georgia.]*
