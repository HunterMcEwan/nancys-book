A sepia-toned vignetted studio head-and-shoulders portrait of a young officer in U.S. Army uniform, smiling broadly at the camera.

He wears the wool overseas cap (garrison cap) bearing twin captain's bars at the front. His tunic carries the lapel disks of a U.S. Army officer: **U.S.** on the right collar and what appears to be the caduceus insignia of the **Medical Corps** on the left, with a smaller caduceus on the lapel. A faint shoulder patch is visible at the lower right of the print.

A pencilled signature or studio mark at the lower right corner of the photograph is illegible at this resolution.

*[No caption is written on the page. The subject is almost certainly James Pickens Walker Jr. (b. 1912), the compiler's son, in his Army Medical Corps uniform — he served as a medical officer during the Second World War. The portrait appears to date from his captaincy, ca. 1942–1945.]*
