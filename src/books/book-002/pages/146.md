Two newspaper clippings pasted side by side at the upper half of the page; both relate to the December 1937 debut of Mary Ann Walker.

**Left clipping (Savannah Morning News, Dec. 2, 1937):**

Pencilled annotations at the top of the clipping:

> Four — *Savannah Morning [News]*
>
> Dec. 2nd 1937

Headline in bold:

> **Colonial Dames House Is [Scene of] Lovely Reception For Miss [Walker]**

Article body (largely legible in fragments):

> At a lovely reception during the late afternoon at the Colonial Dames House, Miss Mary Anne Walker will be introduced by her mother, Mrs. J. Pickens Walker.
>
> The reception beautifully decorated with pink and white roses, dogwood among the trees made to entwine with a tea cloth, and has a centerpiece of mixed carnations with lilies tied in pink ribbon. Mrs. David S. Atkinson and Miss Anne Bridge will pour. Receiving with [the] Misses Anne Raymond V. Harris in evening particular [parties]…
>
> [The clipping continues with a long single-column list of the names of receiving-line guests and assistants. Sample names visible in the column:] Mrs. David S. Atkinson, Mrs. Donald Atkins of Charleston, S. C., Mrs. Jesse Fitzsimons, Mrs. Eric Mathews, Mrs. Florian Plowden, Mrs. William Robinson, Mrs. Hudson Scott, Mrs. Marvin Mathews, Mrs. W. Walter Despeaux, Mrs. Thomas Gibson and Miss Beverly Carolyn Jones serving with Miss Janet Anders and Miss Antoinette Atkinson, Mrs. M. Hall Stanton Free and Miss Virginia Jones.
>
> Miss Walker is wearing a dress of white satin made with a long fitted bodice trimmed with green chiffon at the neckline and short sleeves; the skirt is wearing a corsage of orchids and carrying a bouquet of [white roses].
>
> [Mrs. Walker's dress of pearl blue trimmed with cellulose with brown background. Her corsage was of orchids, and she is wearing a corsage of orchids and a feather bouquet of] white roses.

> Mrs. Walker came from Jacksonville to attend her daughter, and following the reception she is leaving for [home]…

**Right clipping (Union, Jacksonville, Florida, Friday — Dec. 23 [1937]):**

Pencilled annotation at the top edge:

> Dec. 23

Headline:

> **Attractive Debutante 1938**

A full-length studio photograph of a young woman in a long pale formal gown with a sweetheart neckline and a wide off-the-shoulder sash; she holds a large bouquet of [white roses or callas] at her waist. She stands before a draped background flanked by flowers in tall vases.

[The accompanying caption block, in very small type to the right of the photograph, is largely illegible at this scan resolution but appears to identify the subject as Miss Mary Ann Walker, daughter of Mr. and Mrs. J. Pickens Walker.]

*[The two clippings together document the December 1937 debut of Mary Ann Walker — in Savannah at the Colonial Dames House on Dec. 2 (introduced by her mother), and again in Jacksonville on Dec. 23. She is the daughter of the compiler.]*
