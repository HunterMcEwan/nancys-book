A typewritten sheet, the front side of a two-page document. The typing is faded carbon copy. The text continues on page 34 (the reverse).

> Extracts from family Bible of **John Lucius Hedley**, brother of my grandmother, **Maria Ann Hedley**.
>
> ⋯⋯⋯⋯
>
> On blank page in beginning of book is written, "**The Altar bloomed June 14th, 1846, in Broad Street near Friend Street**".
>
> (They owned and occupied then the house now owned by F. E. Heyward on Broad St.)
>
> **Minnie P. FitzSimons — 1924**.

**Married**

> **John L. Hedley** to **Harriet F. Bryant** Nov. 9, 1826.

**Married**

> **Benjamin J. Whaley** to **Sarah Jane Hedley** May 10, 1849.

> **John Lucius Hedley**, the son of the **Rev. Dr. John Hedley, D.D.** was born in [illegible] Town near London, England, May 26, 1802, and came to Charleston with his parents in his infancy. The Rev. John Hedley was married to Miss **Sarah Russell** in **St. Mary's Church, London**. He was the son of **Roger Hedley** of **Collowhill, Northumberland, Eng.**

**Deaths**

> The Rev. **John Hedley, D.D.** about 68 yrs. Father of John L. Hedley died on the end of February 1834. Buried in **St. Michael's church yard**.
>
> **William Hedley**, age 28 yrs. 6 mos. 28 days, brother of J. L. Hedley died on the morning of the 19th of December 1834. Buried in **St. Michael's Church yard**.
>
> **John Lucius Hedley** died on the 5th day of January 1855 after having survived three strokes of paralysis at his residence on 15 King St., in the 53 (53) year of his age.

> **Sisters**: **Eliza Steele**, *née* Hedley, buried in **St. Paul's Church yard**.
> of John
> L. Hedley **Maria Ann Fuller**, *née* Hedley, buried in **church** yard of Baptist
> Church in **Barnwell, S. C.**

> Inserted by **Minnie P. FitzS.**
>
> Births of Negroes belonging to J. L. Hedley and family —
>
> (Faithful and beloved house servants I imagine) MPF 1944
>
> **Selina** daughter **Jimmy** and **Daphne** born 4th March 1841

*[The text continues on the verso, page 34.]*
