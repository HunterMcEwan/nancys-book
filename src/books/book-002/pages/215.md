**Upper right — newspaper clipping.** A small clipped birth notice with a centered headline:

> JAMES WALKER McEWAN
>
> Mr. and Mrs. O. B. McEwan of Orlando, announce the birth of a son, James Walker, on Monday evening, April 26, in Orlando. Mrs. McEwan is the former Miss Mary Ann Walker of Jacksonville.

To the right of the clipping, a pencilled annotation:

> 1948

**Upper centre — photograph.** A black-and-white snapshot of a small child standing in a living room beside a sofa and a lamp on a side table. Captioned in blue ink below:

> Jim in living room — Sussmark[*?*] St.

*[Street name as on page 207 — possibly "Sussmark" or "Sussmaek"; the cursive is consistent with that earlier annotation. Likely the family's pre-Osceola address.]*

**Middle left — photograph.** A black-and-white snapshot of a man in a short-sleeved white shirt and pale slacks holding a fair-haired baby on his right hip on a lawn. Captioned in blue ink below:

> Jim + Bo
>
> O.B. McEwan home
> 1129 S. Osceola St.
> Orlando, Fla.

**Middle right — photograph.** A small black-and-white snapshot, slightly out of focus, of two adults and an infant outdoors against a hazy sky. A pencilled caption beneath, partly illegible:

> [Kids — Park — Jim — H.?]

*[The caption is faint and several characters are uncertain.]*

**Lower right — photograph.** A larger black-and-white photograph mounted at the lower right corner, showing a two-story stucco house with a low-pitched tile or shingle roof, screened porch, and a tall palm tree at the front lawn. No caption beside this print; it corresponds to the Osceola Street address noted in the middle-left caption.
