notes: "Third and final page of Nancy McEwan Green's letter of 3 September 1968 to her uncle and aunt 'Peck and Ann' (James Pickens Walker Jr. and Ann Knight Walker). Nancy describes her brothers Christopher Gaillard McEwan ('Chris') and James W. McEwan ('Jim') arriving home dressed as 'Montana cowboys' (Christopher had been living in Columbia Falls, Montana — see obituary, page 247), notes her concern for her uncle's health (he would die four months later, 2 January 1969 — see page 251), and plans to visit Jacksonville, Florida with the new baby. Signed 'Love, / Nancy' at lower right and dated 'Sept. 3, 1968' at lower left."

The third leaf and conclusion of the letter, in blue ink.

> a thrill to watch the progress he makes each day.
>
> Chris & Jim got home Sat. looking like Montana cowboys — boots, hats, bow ties, etc. Chris flew to Miami yesterday and returns Thurs. and Jim leaves Fri. They are never home for long.
>
> I do hope that Peck is feeling better. He is constantly in our thoughts and prayers.
>
> We plan to come to Jax. with the baby sometime real soon.
>
> Love,
>
> Nancy

Dated at lower left:

> Sept. 3, 1968

*[Chris = Christopher Gaillard McEwan (see graduation announcement, page 264; he had been living in Columbia Falls, Montana, accounting for the 'Montana cowboys' joke). Jim = James W. McEwan. 'Peck' (here spelled out) is the writer's uncle J. Pickens Walker Jr., who was gravely ill and would die on 2 January 1969 — see obituary, page 251. 'Jax.' = Jacksonville.]*
