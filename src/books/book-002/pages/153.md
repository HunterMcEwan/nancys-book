notes: "Continuation sheet of a letter from 'Julia' (widow of Coburn 'Pick' Walker) dated Feb. 10, 1945 (signed on page 156). Written in blue ink in a flowing hand on cream paper. Julia reflects on her late husband's work — knowing how hard he had worked and how much of himself he had put into it — and reports that the promotion was to have come through Mr. MacKay. She mentions a lovely letter from 'Mrs. Pickens', plans to visit, and the receipt yesterday of word of 'Cohun's Aunt Jessie's' death."

…several months. Knowing how hard he had worked and how much of himself he had put into his work, it made me almost sick to think that he hadn't known about it. It would have pleased him to ~~have~~ had his ability recognized — I think it *was* recognized and appreciated by all of them individually — but of course, the promotion had to come through Mr. MacKay —

Amy, Mrs. Pickens wrote that she had such a lovely letter from you — and seemed greatly comforted by it —

As soon as the weather is [milder] I am planning to go out to see them — But am afraid it will have to be from Wilmington rather than from here —
