The writer describes the elaborate mock-cocktail she serves her sick young son Puck — flat ginger ale, ice, a dash of water, and 'half a small jigger of bourbon' — and teases 'Tressie' for being the original source of Puck's taste in drinks. Closes with the family doctor's verdict that 'Puck is going purely on will power.' Blue fountain-pen cursive on ruled letter paper."

> get it good & flat. Then into a cheese glass goes half a small jigger of bourbon, 1 piece of ice, a dash of water for melted ice, and flat ginger ale. He thinks it's wonderful. There are two things, Tressie, you have to answer for — (1) How did he know about your drinks; (2) don't you think it's a terrible heritage to give a child — a taste for flat ginger ale? You'll never hear the last of that one!!
